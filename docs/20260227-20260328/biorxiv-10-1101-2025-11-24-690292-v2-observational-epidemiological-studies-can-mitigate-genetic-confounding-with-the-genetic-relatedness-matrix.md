## 摘要
观察性研究在心理学和流行病学中常用于识别与健康结果相关的风险因素。然而，当共享的遗传变异同时影响推定的风险因素和结果时，这些研究容易受到混杂因素的影响。研究人员历来使用多基因评分（polygenic scores）来控制此类遗传混杂，但这些评分通常是性状遗传成分的含噪声且有偏的估计量。在本文中，我们开发了一种利用遗传亲缘关系矩阵（GRM）的方法，在测试非遗传风险因素时控制遗传混杂。在模拟实验中，我们发现我们的方法优于现有方法，特别是在样本量对于许多人类研究标准而言较大、但小于人类遗传学常用数据集的情况下。我们还证明了现有方法容易受到全基因组关联研究（GWAS）可移植性差的影响，而我们的方法在本质上对这些问题具有鲁棒性。最后，我们将该方法应用于英国生物样本库（UK Biobank），重新分析了先前研究不足的队列中健康结果的社会风险因素。

意义声明：大型观察性数据集常用于流行病学或社会科学，以识别与健康结果相关的风险因素。然而，当推定的风险因素和结果都是复杂的遗传性状时，这些研究可能会产生误导：相关的遗传基础可能会在推定的风险因素和结果之间产生虚假关联。解决这一问题的现有方法通常依赖于 GWAS 汇总统计数据，这需要生物样本库规模的数据集。在这项工作中，我们介绍了一种替代方法，使用遗传亲缘关系矩阵（GRM）直接控制遗传混杂。我们表明，我们的方法与现有方法密切相关，但在较小的数据集中表现更好，这使其成为研究未被充分研究的性状和人群的研究人员特别有价值的工具。

## Abstract
Observational studies are commonly used in psychology and epidemiology to identify risk factors correlated with health outcomes. However, these studies are vulnerable to confounding when shared genetic variation influences both the putative risk factor and outcome. Researchers have historically controlled for this type of genetic confounding using polygenic scores, but these scores are often noisy and biased estimators of a traits genetic component. Here, we develop a method that leverages a genetic relatedness matrix (GRM) to control genetic confounding when testing for non-genetic risk factors. In simulations, we find that our method outperforms existing approaches, particularly at sample sizes that are large by the standards of much human research but smaller than datasets often used in human genetics. We also demonstrate that existing methods are susceptible to poor GWAS portability, whereas our method is inherently robust to such concerns. Finally, we apply our method to the UK Biobank to re-analyze social risk factors for health outcomes in previously understudied cohorts.

Significance statementLarge observational datasets are often used in epidemiology or social sciences to identify risk factors associated with health outcomes. However, these studies can be misleading when the putative risk factor and outcome are both complex genetic traits: a correlated genetic basis can create spurious associations between a putative risk factor and outcome. Existing approaches to address this problem typically rely on GWAS summary statistics, which require biobank-scale datasets. In this work, we introduce an alternative method that uses a genetic relatedness matrix (GRM) to control for genetic confounding directly. We show that our approach is closely related to existing methods but outperforms them in smaller datasets, making it an especially valuable tool for researchers who work on understudied traits and populations.
