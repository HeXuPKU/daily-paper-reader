## 摘要
免疫细胞组成的遗传调控在复杂疾病的病因学中起着至关重要的作用，但目前对其了解仍然有限。我们提出了一个统一的分析框架，将细胞类型比例的全基因组关联研究（GWAS）与细胞类型范围关联研究（cWAS）相结合，系统地表征免疫细胞组成的遗传调控及其对疾病风险的下游影响。利用来自 OneK1K 队列的单细胞 RNA 测序数据，我们采用针对有界、过度离散特征设计的深度加权拟二项模型，对免疫细胞类型比例进行了 GWAS。我们确定了 47 个全基因组显著位点，这些位点影响 8 个精细标记的免疫细胞亚型。利用这些已识别的遗传效应，我们进一步使用基于多基因风险评分（PRS）的推断方法计算了遗传调节比例（GRPs），并通过 cWAS 评估了它们与复杂疾病的关联。我们确定了 5 个显著的细胞类型-疾病关联，包括 2 个与 1 型糖尿病相关，2 个与克罗恩病相关，以及 1 个与溃疡性结肠炎相关。总之，我们的结果表明，在 scRNA-seq 中观察到的细胞类型比例可以揭示调控位点，并为遗传变异如何调节免疫细胞类型比例以影响疾病风险提供见解。尽管我们专注于免疫单细胞数据，但随着 scRNA-seq 数据集的扩展，我们的框架也适用于其他组织或细胞组成。

作者总结：全基因组关联研究（GWAS）已经发现了许多与疾病相关的信号，但大多数位于非编码区且难以解释。因此，将 GWAS 信号映射到相关的细胞类型对于更好地理解驱动疾病的生物学机制至关重要。一个主要的挑战是，观察到的基因表达和测量的细胞类型比例可能受到环境因素和疾病状态的影响。相比之下，基因型受这些因素的影响较小，因此在解释疾病因素方面更为可靠。此外，细胞类型比例是有界的且通常是偏态的，因此依赖高斯假设的标准 GWAS 模型可能会效力不足。为了解决这个问题，我们开发了一种拟二项方法，该方法能更好地匹配数据，并在控制假阳性的同时提高发现率。在真实数据中，我们的方法比传统的线性模型识别出更多与细胞类型比例相关的遗传位点。为了进一步研究遗传变异如何调节免疫细胞组成以影响疾病风险，我们将研究结果与疾病 GWAS 汇总统计数据相结合，以识别可能导致疾病易感性的免疫细胞类型。总之，我们的结果将疾病相关的 GWAS 信号与特定的免疫细胞类型联系起来，并为这些疾病背后的细胞机制提供了见解。

## Abstract
Genetic regulation of immune cell composition plays a crucial role in the etiology of complex diseases, yet remains poorly understood. We propose a unified analytical framework that integrates genome-wide association studies (GWAS) of cell type proportions with cell-type-wide association studies (cWAS) to systematically characterize both the genetic regulation of immune cell composition and its downstream effects on disease risk. Using single-cell RNA sequencing data from the OneK1K cohort, we conducted a GWAS of immune cell-type proportions with a depth-weighted quasi-binomial model designed for bounded, overdispersed traits. We identified 47 genome-wide significant loci influencing eight fine-labeled immune cell subtypes. Leveraging these identified genetic effects, we further imputed genetically regulated proportions (GRPs) using polygenic risk score (PRS)-based imputation and assessed their associations with complex diseases through cWAS. We identified five significant cell type-disease associations, including two with type 1 diabetes, two with Crohns disease, and one with ulcerative colitis. Together, our results demonstrate that cell type proportions observed in scRNA-seq can reveal regulatory loci and offer insights into how genetic variations regulate immune cell type proportions to affect disease risk. Although we focused on immune single-cell data, our framework is applicable to other tissues or cellular compositions as scRNA-seq datasets expand.

Author SummaryGenome-wide association studies (GWASs) have uncovered many disease-associated signals, yet most lie in noncoding regions and are difficult to interpret. Mapping GWAS signals to the relevant cell types is therefore important for better understanding the biological mechanisms that drive disease. A major challenge is that observed gene expression and measured cell-type proportions can be influenced by environmental factors and disease status. In contrast, genotypes are less affected by these factors, making them more reliable for interpreting factors of diseases. Moreover, the cell-type proportions are bounded and often skewed, so standard GWAS models that rely on Gaussian assumptions may lose power. To address this, we developed a quasi-binomial approach that better matches the data and improves discovery while controlling false positives. In real data, our method identified more genetic loci associated with cell-type proportions than a traditional linear model. To further investigate how genetic variation regulates immune cell composition to influence disease risk, we integrated our results with disease GWAS summary statistics to identify immune cell types that may contribute to disease susceptibility. Together, our results link disease-associated GWAS signals to specific immune cell types and provide insights into the cellular mechanisms that may underlie these diseases.
