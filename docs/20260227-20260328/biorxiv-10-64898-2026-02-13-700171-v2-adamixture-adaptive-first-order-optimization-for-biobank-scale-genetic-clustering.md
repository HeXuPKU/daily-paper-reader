## 摘要
动机：从测序数据中估计遗传聚类是群体遗传学和医学遗传学中的一项基础任务，能够实现人口统计学推断并在关联研究中对群体结构进行调整。ADMIXTURE 是一种广泛使用的基于模型的聚类方法，它采用加速期望最大化（EM）算法来推断群体参数；然而，其计算需求扩展性较差，限制了其在现代生物样本库规模数据集中的应用。虽然最近采用二阶拟牛顿方案的 EM 加速策略保持了准确性，但它们仍然具有较高的计算强度。相反，优先考虑速度的非 EM 方法往往会牺牲解的质量。结果：我们推出了 ADAMIXTURE，这是一种将 EM 算法与自适应矩估计（Adam）相结合的新型优化框架。与传统的加速方法不同，ADAMIXTURE 利用一阶梯度以及源自一阶矩和二阶矩的自适应学习率来近似曲率信息，从而绕过了 Hessian 近似带来的计算开销。该方法在保持一阶更新的低计算复杂度的同时，超越了二阶方法的收敛效率。在模拟和大规模实证数据集上，与现有最先进的方法相比，ADAMIXTURE 在保持相当或更高推断准确性的同时，显著减少了实际运行时间并增强了可扩展性。其 GPU 实现在 50 万个样本和变异位点上运行时间不到 2 小时，比当前最先进的方法快了两个数量级。源代码可在以下网址获取：https://github.com/AI-sandbox/ADAMIXTURE

## Abstract
Motivation: Estimating genetic clusters from sequencing data is a fundamental task in population and medical genetics, enabling demographic inference and adjustment for population structure in association studies. ADMIXTURE, a widely used model-based clustering method, employs an accelerated Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm to infer population parameters; however, its computational demands scale poorly, limiting its usefulness for modern biobank-sized datasets. While recent EM acceleration strategies employing second-order quasi-Newton schemes preserve accuracy, they remain computationally intensive. Conversely, EM-free approaches that prioritize speed often compromise solution quality. Results: We introduce ADAMIXTURE, a novel optimization framework that integrates the EM algorithm with Adaptive Moment Estimation (Adam). Unlike traditional acceleration methods, ADAMIXTURE utilizes first-order gradients with adaptive learning rates derived from raw and squared moments to approximate curvature information, bypassing the computational overhead of Hessian approximations. This approach surpasses the convergence efficiency of second-order methods while maintaining the low computational complexity of first-order updates. Across simulated and large-scale empirical datasets, ADAMIXTURE demonstrates substantial reductions in wall-clock runtime and enhanced scalability compared to state-of-the-art methods, while maintaining comparable or improved inference accuracy. Its GPU implementation runs in under 2 hours on half a million samples and variants, a two order of magnitude speedup over current state-of-the-art. Source code is available at: https://github.com/AI-sandbox/ADAMIXTURE
