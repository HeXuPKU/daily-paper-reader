Abstract 14 

Long-read sequencing is a powerful technique capturing multiple variants within single continuous 15 

reads. This length allows individual reads to bridge small and structural variants while carrying 16 

crucial phasing information. However, current computational tools treat small variant calling, 17 

structural variant (SV) detection and phasing as largely disconnected problems, failing to unleash 18 

the full potential of long reads. Here, we present longcallD, a unified framework utilizing local 19 

multiple-sequence alignment to simultaneously call and phase small and structural variants. By 20 

integrating germline phasing and retrotransposition hallmarks, longcallD also identifies low-21 

fraction mosaic variants and detects mobile element insertions supported by a single read. 22 

Compared to existing methods, our unified approach substantially improves SV discovery and 23 

mosaic variants accuracy while maintaining competitive small variant calling. We anticipate that 24 

longcallD will provide a robust foundation for resolving complex genetic architectures in clinical 25 

and evolutionary applications. 26

The advent of 31 

PacBio high-fidelity (HiFi) sequencing and ONT R10.4.1 chemistry has further improved base-32 

level read accuracy, substantially reducing sequencing errors and enhancing variant calling 33 

performance 3,4 . Despite these progresses, accurately calling the full spectrum of genetic variation 34 

that encompasses single-nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs), small insertions and deletions 35 

(indels), and large structural variants (SVs) from long-read data remains a substantial 36 

computational challenge. 37 

With short reads, small variant calling, SV calling and phasing are three independent 38 

problems as a standard short read rarely bridges ≥2 variants or spans an entire SV 5. These 39 

problems are thus solved by distinct algorithms. In contrast, a single long read often connects 40 

multiple small and structural variants and naturally encodes their phasing. Long reads have 41 

unified small/structural variant calling and phasing but this advantage has not been fully utilized. 42 

On small variant calling, modern long-read callers including longshot 6, Clair3 7 and 43 

DeepVariant 8,9 either natively implement a phasing algorithm or use a third-party phasing tool. 44 

However, unaware of SVs, they are sensitive to inconsistent alignment around SVs, especially in 45 

low-complexity regions (LCRs), which may lead to spurious variant calls 2,10 . Clair3 and 46 

DeepVariant rely on deep learning to filter such errors but without multiple-sequence 47 

realignment, they are unable to recover small variants tangled with SVs and thus lose power. 48 

On SV calling, early tools such that DELLY 11 , pbsv, DeBreak 12 , SVIM 13 and cuteSV 14 

49 

ignore read phasing. SVDSS 15 and sawfish 16 apply local realignment or reassembly to phase 50 

close SVs but do not leverage phasing in entire reads. Sniffles2 17 and Severus 18 optionally use 51 

full read phasing derived from third-party tools but without realigning or reassembling reads, 52 

they often fail to resolve SVs in LCRs; false SNP calls caused by SVs in turn reduce phasing 53 

accuracy and subsequently SV accuracy as well. Sniffles2 is the only SV caller that is developed 54 

with mosaic SV calling in mind, but it produced hundreds of false mosaic SV calls as we will 55 

show later. In summary, although modern variant callers use phasing to some extent, none of 56 

them tightly integrates small variant calling, SV calling and phasing. 57 

To address these challenges, we developed longcallD, a unified framework for the joint 58 

calling and phasing of small variants and SVs from long genomic reads. LongcallD explicitly 59 

distinguishes clean and noisy genomic regions, applies haplotype-aware multiple sequence 60 

alignment (MSA) within noisy regions to derive consensus sequences, and integrates clean- and 61

Results 69 

Overview of longcallD 70 

Given a reference genome and a long-read alignment, longcallD produces phased variant calls and 71 

can optionally output phased long reads with refined alignments. The overall workflow is 72 

illustrated in Fig. 1 , with algorithmic details described in Methods. 73 

LongcallD is motivated by the widespread presence of heterozygous SNPs and small indels 74 

in human genomes 5 and by the observation that variant calling errors in long reads are highly 75 

enriched in specific genomic contexts. LongcallD identifies noisy regions that comprise (i) 76 

homopolymers and short tandem repeats (STRs), where sequencing errors occur more frequently; 77 

(ii) Variable number tandem repeats (VNTR), where read alignments are highly inconsistent 19 ; and 78 

(iii) large insertion or deletion SV regions, where alignments frequently exhibit clipping near SV 79 

boundaries. The remaining regions are designated as clean, in which read alignments are generally 80 

accurate and concordant. High-confidence germline SNPs and small indels are called directly from 81 

clean regions by allele counting. 82 

LongcallD then exploits heterozygous variants in clean regions (allele fraction 0.2–0.8) to 83 

locally partition long reads into haplotypes. For each noisy region, haplotype-specific read 84 

subsequences are extracted and subjected to MSA to derive haplotype-resolved consensus 85 

sequences. In regions lacking nearby heterozygous variants, longcallD performs haplotype-86 

agnostic MSA followed by sequence clustering to distinguish heterozygous from homozygous 87 

regions. Consensus sequences are realigned to the reference to generate variant calls, which are 88 

subsequently integrated with variants in clean regions through an iterative haplotype-extension 89 

procedure to produce jointly phased calls across the genome. 90 

Beyond germline variants, longcallD also calls mosaic variants of low VAF. By leveraging 91 

established haplotype information together with a stringent, context-aware filtering strategy, 92

Benchmar k datasets and evaluation framework for germline variant s116 

We evaluated germline small - and structural -variant calling performance using the Genome -in -a-117 

Bottle (GIAB) whole -genome assembly -based benchmark constructed from the T2T HG002 Q100 118 

v1.1 diploid assembly 10 . The GRCh38 version of this benchmark contain s 4.6 million small 119 

variants and 2 8,246 large indels . Compared to the previous HG002 SV benchmark v0.6 21 , T2T 120 

Q100 v1.1 includes roughly twice as many SVs, with the majority of newly added variants located 121 

in LCRs, which are known to be challenging for variant calling 2,10 .122 

We used o ne PacBio HiFi (63x) and one ONT R10.4 .1 (50x) dataset of the HG002 sample 123 

for evaluation. For each dataset, we generated de novo assemblies using long reads only with 124 

hifiasm 22,23 and additionally included the T2T HG002 assembly for comparison . Long reads and 125 

assemblies were aligned to GRCh38, and variants were called from both read alignments and 126 

assembly alignments. LongcallD was compared with two long -read small variant callers (Clair3 7

> 127

and DeepVariant 9), eight long -read SV callers (cuteSV 14 , DeBreak 12 , DELLY 11 , pbsv 24 , sawfish 16 ,128 

Sniffles2 17 , SVDSS 15 , and SVIM 13 ), and four assembly -based variant callers (cuteSV -asm, i.e., 129 

cuteSV in assembly mode 14 , dipcall 25 , PAV 26 , and SVIM -asm 13 ). Among these, longcallD, dipcall ,130 

and PAV report both small variants and SVs. 131 

We measure the s mall -variant accuracy using hap.py 27 , and the SV accuracy using 132 

Truvari 28 . However, upon examining the Truvari reports, we found that Truvari does not require 133 

exact allele matches, leading to a substantial underestimation of SV errors . To better characterize 134 

SV calling performance in LCRs, we additionally used aardvark 29 , a haplotype -centric 135 

benchmarking tool that directly compares haplotype sequences rather than variant representations. 136 

Aardvark reports both genotype -level scores and basepair -level scores, the latter allowing partial 137 

credit for sequence -level concordance which is an essential property for benchmarking in LCRs. 138 Haplotype tagging of noisy regions via flanking heterozygous variants 139 

We first evaluated the effectiveness of noisy -region classification and local haplotype partitioning 140 

of longcallD . Only noisy regions overlapping at least one benchmark variant were considered. For 141 

HiFi data, 93.4% (673,943 of 721,633) of noisy regions were flanked by at least one heterozygous 142 

clean -region variant, enabling haplotype -aware separation of long reads ( Fig. 2a ). For ONT data, 143 

this fraction increased to 96.3% (738,826 of 766,898) due to the longer ONT read s which span 144 

informative heterozygous sites over larger distances .145 

We next examined the length distribution of noisy regions ( Fig. 2b ). Haplotype -aware and 146 

haplotype -agnostic noisy regions exhibited similar length distributions for both technologies, with 147 

a pronounced peak at approximately 40 bp. This peak corresponds primarily to homopolymer -148 

associated regions consisting of ~20 -bp homopolymers flanked by two additional 10 -bp windows. 149 

Although long noisy regions constitute a minority, they are strongly enriched for tandem repeats: 150 

53.9% of HiFi noisy regions ≥200 bp and 58.6% of ONT noisy regions ≥120 bp overlap annotated 151 

tandem repeats. These results demonstrate that longcallD effectively leverages long -range 152 

haplotype information to resolve most challenging regions encountered in long -read data. 153 

Comparable small -variant calling accuracy to state -of -the -art methods 154 

We extracted SNPs and small indels (<50 bp) from variant calls generated by longcallD, PAV, and 155 

dipcall, and evaluated them together with DeepVariant and Clair3 against the ground -truth call set .156 

Accuracy was assessed using precision, recall, F1 score, false -negative rate (FNR), and false 157 

positives per million bases (FPPM), with results stratified into homopolymer and non -158 

homopolymer regions based on GIAB genome stratification v3.6 30 .159 

Using HiFi reads, all callers exhibited higher error rates in homopolymer regions than in 160 

non -homopolymer regions ( Fig. 2c,d ). Among alignment -based callers, longcallD achieved the 161 

lowest FNRs and highest F1 scores for both SNPs and indels across all confident regions 162 

(Supplementary Fig. 1a,b ). LongcallD produced fewer false positives and false negatives for 163 

SNPs in homopolymer regions , potentially due to MSA , whereas DeepVariant achieved higher 164 

accuracy for homopolymer indels probably because it models the homopolymer error profile better 165 

with deep learning .166 

Assembly -based callers dipcall and PAV showed comparable SNP calling accuracy 167 

between HiFi and T2T assemblies; however, hifiasm HiFi assembly carried noticeably more indel 168 

error s especially in homopolymer regions, while the T2T assembly did not have this issue . Relative 169 to dipcall and PAV applied to the HiFi assembly, longcallD exhibited a lower FNR but a higher 170 

FPPM. Overall, with HiFi long reads, longcallD demonstrated small variant calling performance 171 

comparable to both alignment -based and assembly -based state -of -the -art methods. 172 

With ONT long reads, all callers called substantially more homopolymer SNP and indel 173 

error s compared to HiFi data ( Fig. 2c –f). LongcallD achieved the lowest FNRs for both SNPs and 174 

indels in non -homopolymer regions, whereas DeepVariant and Clair3 showed superior 175

For more precise SV evaluation at the haplotype level , we applied aardvark 29 to compute sequence -220 

based genotype - and basepair -level F1 scores. Aardvark jointly considers small and structural 221 

variants. However, e xcept for longcallD, dipcall, and PAV, other SV callers do not output small 222 

variants, which would lead to inherent bias in aardvark -based evaluation. For these SV -only callers, 223 

SNPs and small indels (<30 bp) from DeepVariant were merged with SV calls for fair comparison .224 

Although this solution is not ideal due to differen t variant representations , it is the best we could 225 

do with these long -read SV callers. 226 

Across both metrics, longcallD consistently achieved higher F1 scores than other long -read 227 

SV callers, particularly in tandem repeat s (Fig. 4a,b ). Compared with assembly -based callers, 228 

longcallD exhibited accuracy comparable to dipcall on HiFi data and higher accuracy on ONT 229 

data. PAV consistently achieved higher basepair F1 scores but lower genotype F1 scores than 230 

longcallD , reflecting differences in sequence accuracy versus allelic representation . Overall, 231 

longcallD, dipcall, and PAV achieved similar accuracy on both HiFi and ONT datasets and 232 

substantially outperformed other SV callers. 233 

To further characterize performance in tandem repeat regions, we examined region -wise 234 

F1 scores computed by aardvark for tandem repeat regions containing variant sequences of ≥ 50 235 

bp. Region -wise F1 scores were calculated using all small and structural variants within each 236 

region, providing an aggregate measure of haplotype -level concordance between variant calls and 237 

the ground truth. For HiFi data, longcallD achieved perfect genotype and basepair F1 scores of 1.0 238 

in 80.8% (13,294 of 16,452) and 81.9% (13,471 of 16,452) of regions, respectively. Similar 239 

fractions were observed for ONT data (81.2% for genotype and 81.9% for basepair) . Across all 240 

genotype - and basepair -level F1 thresholds, longcallD consistently yielded a higher proportion of 241 

high -scoring regions than other long -read SV callers (Fig. 4c,d) . Although PAV and dipcall 242 

exhibited slightly lower proportions of perfectly concordant regions, dipcall shows the highest 243 

precisions and PAV shows the highest recalls across most of the thresholds (Supplementary Fig. 244 

4). Overall, the performance of longcallD, PAV, and dipcall substantially exceeded that of other 245 

long -read SV calling methods .246 

We also examined region -wise accuracy using the original variant calls from long -read SV 247 

callers ( Supplementary Fig. 5). The basepair -level metrics of aardvark evaluate similarity at the 248 

base level, leading to high scores for highly similar sequences, whereas genotype -level metrics 249 require exact matches for variants within the region . As expected, not jointly calling the small 250 

variants in the regions , all the other long -read SV callers have noticeably lower recalls , as they do 251 

not report nearby small variants . For precisions, they exhibited higher basepair -level precision s252 

than genotype -level precisions, indicating the sequences of called variants are highly similar to the 253 

ground truth but not enough for haplotype reconstruction. 254 

These results indicate that joint ly calling small and large variants is critical for accurate 255 

haplotype reconstruction in repetitive genomic contexts . Fig. 5 shows a representative tandem -256 

repeat region on chromosome 5 in which longcallD correctly resolved three large insertions on 257 

both haplotype s, despite extensive clipping and fragmented , inconsistent alignments in the raw 258 

data. Through haplotype -aware alignment refinement, longcallD produced internally consistent 259 

haplotype -specific representations that exactly matched the ground truth . Across all evaluated 260 

datasets, longcallD, PAV, and dipcall achieved perfect genotype - and basepair -level F1 scores for 261 

this region, whereas SV -only callers generally yielded F1 scores below 0.80 . An exception was 262 

SVDSS, which achieved a high basepair F1 score (0.99) but a substantially lower genotype F1 263 

score (0.50) on the HiFi dataset. 264

Mosaic SNV detection 282 

We next evaluated longcallD’s performance in detecting mosaic SNVs of low VAF .283 

DeepSomatic 31 (tumor -only mode) and Clair -Mosaic 32 were included for comparison. We first 284 

analyzed a COLO829 (melanoma) and COLO829BL (matched normal) mixture generated by the 285 

SMaHT network 33 at a low tumor:normal ratio of 1:49 . This design preserves the original 286 

germline haplotypes while introducing COLO829 -specific somatic mutations at expected VAFs 287 

of ~1% (heterozygous) or ~2% (homozygous). We focused on a high -confidence reference set of 288 

28,603 mosaic SNVs located in “easy” regions defined by the 1000 Genomes strict mask 5. These 289 

truth SNVs were produced by SMaHT from high -coverage tumor -normal pairs. 290 

On a 110× HiFi dataset, longcallD achieved the highest precision (52.0%) and recall 291 

(33.1%), outperforming DeepSomatic (47.6% precision, 20.7% recall) and Clair -Mosaic (17.2% 292 

precision, 3.4% recall) ( Fig. 6a ). LongcallD uniquely identified 4,343 true positive SNVs not 293 

detected by the other callers, the majority of which were supported by only two alternative reads 294 

(Fig. 6 b). The alternative allele depth distribution of longcallD -specific calls was significantly 295 

lower than that of calls reported by DeepSomatic or Clair -Mosaic (P < 2.2 × 10 -16 ), indicating 296 

superior sensitivity for variants with minimal read support. The relatively low recall observed for 297 

all methods is expected, as many mosaic SNVs with VAFs of 1 –2% are not sampled even at 298 

110× coverage under binomial sampling constraints. On an 87 -fold ONT dataset of the same 299 

COLO829 mixture sample, while longcallD and DeepSomatic show a similar recall (15.3% and 300 

15.8%) which are both lower than that on HiFi dataset, longcallD shows a superior precision of 301 

39.0% compared to 5.6% for DeepSomatic (Sup plementary Fig. 6 ).302 

To assess performance at moderate VAFs, we generated a synthetic 80× HiFi dataset by 303 

mixing H2009 (lung cancer) and H2009BL (matched normal) cell lines at a tumor:normal ratio 304 

of 1:10. A published high -confidence somatic SNV call set comprising 140,043 variants 305 

supported by multiple sequencing technologies 28 served as the ground truth. Clair -Mosaic was 306 

excluded from this analysis because it failed to report any mosaic SNVs on this dataset. Both 307 

longcallD and DeepSomatic achieved substantially higher precision (94.6% and 95.7%, 308 

respectively; Fig. 6c ) than in the 1:49 mixture. However, longcallD showed slightly lower recall 309 

(66.8%) than DeepSomatic (72.9%), indicating that DeepSomatic is more sensitive for mosaic 310 

SNVs with moderate VAFs and stronger read support. 311 Mosaic large indel detection 312 

We next evaluated mosaic large indel detection using the synthetic H2009 dataset , with Sniffles2 313 

(mosaic mode) included for comparison. As no curated ground -truth large -indel set is available 314 

for this dataset, we constructed an integrated reference set from four somatic SV callers 315 

including Severus 18 , SAVANA 34 , nanomonsv 35 , and mini sv 36 , and a pplied to both tumor and 316 

matched normal samples (Methods). After excluding indels <100 bp, >50 kb or located in 317 

centromeric regions, 661 large indels detected by at least one caller were used to estimate 318 

precision, and 509 indels detected by all four callers were used to estimate recall. 319 

Although longcallD (614) and Sniffles2 (610) reported similar numbers of mosaic large 320 

indels ( ≥100 bp), longcallD achieved substantially higher recall (78.2% versus 46.8%) ( Fig. 6d ). 321 

Whereas Sniffles2 requires at least three supporting reads for mosaic SV detection, longcallD 322 

accurately identified true mosaic indels supported by only one or two reads ( Fig. 6e ), while 323 

maintaining higher overall precision (74.4% versus 46.6%). These results indicate that 324 

haplotype -aware filtering enables sensitive detection of low -VAF mosaic indels without a 325 

corresponding increase in false positives. 326 

We further constructed a mosaic MEI reference set using mini sv on the tumor and normal 327 

H2009 samples. Insertions exhibiting canonical MEI features , i.e., TS D ≥2 bp and poly(A) tail 328 

≥10 bp , and supported by at l east one read in the synthetic HiFi dataset were retained, yielding 329 

467 mosaic MEIs as ground truth. Applying the same TSD and poly(A) criteria to longcallD 330 

calls, we observed a precision of 95.5% and recall of 92.7%, demonstrating that explicit 331 

modeling of MEI sequence features markedly improves detection accuracy for low -VAF 332 

insertions (Fig. 6d) .333 

As a negative control, we called mosaic SVs from HG002 HiFi reads and remapped all 334 

supporting reads to the HG002 T2T Q100 v1.1 assemblies. Supporting r eads were aligned 335 

separately to the two T2T haplotypes and considered validated only if both haplotype alignments 336 

co ntained the corresponding indel. Mosaic SVs were classified as none validated (no supporting 337 

reads validated ), partially validated ( ≥1 but not all supporting reads validated) , or fully validated 338 

(all supporting reads validated ). In total, 69 and 447 mosaic large indels ( ≥100 bp , <50 kb , non -339 

centromeric) were detected by longcall D and Sniffles2, respectively. Among these, only a single 340 

mosaic deletion of 1474 bp (Supplementary Fig. 7 ) with five supporting reads was fully 341 

validated , and it was detected by both callers (Fig. 6f ). Beyond this , m ost events were not 342 validated in the T2T assemblies, considered as false positives . LongcallD reported substantially 343 

fewer such false positive calls, supporting its lower false -positive rate in mosaic SV detection. 344 

We suspect the longcallD false positives are caused by false mappings against the reference 345 

genome due to copy -number variants longer than reads. 346

Discussion 375 

We present longcallD, a unified framework for joint ly calling and phasing small variants, structural 376 

variants and low -VAF mosaic mutations from long -read sequencing data. Across HiFi and ONT 377 

datasets benchmarked against the HG002 T2T Q100 v1.1 truth set, longcallD achieves competitive 378 

small -variant accuracy and the strongest performance among alignment -based SV callers in 379 

tandem repeat s, where variant representation and phasing are most error -prone. 380 

A central design principle of longcallD is the explicit separation of the genome into clean 381 

and noisy regions. Rather than applying a uniform probabilistic model genome -wide, longcallD 382 

performs direct read -count –based calling in well -behaved regions and haplotype -aware MSA –383 

based consensus reconstruction in low -complexity and breakpoint -associated regions . This 384 

targeted strategy addresses the non -uniform error landscape of long -read data and substantially 385 

improves SV detection in tandem repeats. Haplotype -level benchmarking with aardvark further 386 

demonstrates that accurate reconstruction of repeat loci requires joint modeling of small and large 387 

variants. SV -only approaches frequently produce internally inconsistent haplotypes, even when 388 

supplemented with small variants from external callers . In contrast , longcallD, PAV and dipcall, 389 

which natively call both variant classes, achieve markedly higher region -wise genotype 390 

concordance. 391 

Compared to assembly -based approaches, longcallD attains broadly comparable 392 

haplotype -level accuracy while operating directly on read alignments . Using 16 threads on a 393 

standard computational server , longcallD completed analysis of a 40 x HiFi alignment file in 19 394 

minutes with less than 20GB peak memory , compared with 2 18 minutes and 40GB peak memory 395 

for a pipeline comprising DeepVariant, HiPhase and Sniffles2 in phase mode for calling phased 396 

small variants and SVs . De novo assembly -based framework s typically demand several -fold 397 

greater runtime and memory . The design of longcallD eliminates the computational overhead and 398 

assembly -specific biases associated with de novo assembly, particularly in homopolymer -rich 399 

contexts on ONT data . Assembly -based methods retain advantages in more complex repeat 400 

structures , such as segmental duplications , but longcallD provides a practical alternative for 401 

routine analysis at standard whole -genome coverage. 402 

LongcallD further extends haplotype -aware modeling to detect mosaic variant s of low 403 

VAF , which are obscured or absent in assembled contigs . By requiring haplotype consistency 404 

among supporting reads, longcallD improves discrimination between true low -VAF mutations and 405 sequencing artifacts. In tumor –normal mixture experiments at ~1 –2% VAF, longcallD achieved 406 

the highest combined precision and recall for mosaic SNVs and substantially outperformed 407 

existing tools for mosaic large indels, detecting events supported by as few as one or two reads 408 

without a commensurate increase in false positives. These results underscore the value of 409 

integrating germline phasing information into somatic variant inference. 410 

Several limitations of longcallD remain. Homopolymer indel calling , especially on ONT 411 

data, is not as accurate as deep -learning -based methods. Complex SV events such as inversions 412 

and translocations which are more often to occur in tumor samples are not yet supported. Mosaic 413 

small indels are not currently reported due to their overlap with long -read error modes. Future 414 

work will extend variant classes, incorporate learned error models to improve homopolymer indel 415 

calling, and assess performance across diverse human genomes and tumor contexts. 416 

As long -read sequencing becomes increasingly adopted in research and clinical genomics, 417 

unified frameworks that jointly resolve the full spectrum of variation with haplotype context will 418 

be essential. LongcallD provides an integrated solution for germline and mosaic variant analysis 419 

from long -read data, particularly in repetitive regions where accurate haplotype reconstruction is 420 

critical. 421 

> 422

Methods 423 

Identification of variant -calling noisy regions 424 

Collecting candidate noisy regions. LongcallD processes all primary alignments with mapping 425 

quality ≥30. All bases with quality scores ≥10 that diff er from the reference, including substitutions ,426 

insertions, and deletions extracted from CIGAR strings, are treated as candidate variants. 427 

LongcallD identifies noisy regions and records them as reference -coordinate intervals, each 428 

associated with a putative variant size V, which is later used for region merging. Four types of 429 

noisy regions are defined: 430 

1. High -density difference windows. For each primary alignment, longcallD applies an exten si ble 431 

sliding window to count the total number of differing bases between the read and the reference. 432 

A window with length ≥ w bp (100 for HiFi reads and 25 for ONT reads ) and containing more 433 

than five differing bases is classified as a noisy region. The putative variant size V is defined 434 

as the total number of differing bases within the window. 435 

2. Variants in low -complexity regions (LCRs) . Any candidate variant overlapping a n LCR is 436 

classified as noisy. LCRs are identified by applying the sdust algorithm 37 with parameters 437 

“W=20, T=5” to the reference sequence . A noisy region is defined as the variant interval itself ,438 

and the putative variant size V is set to the number of differing bases in the variant. 439 

3. Overlapping candidate variants. If two candidate variants overlap on the reference genome, 440 

their union interval is designated as a noisy region . The putative variant size V is defined as 441 

the larger number of differing bases between the overlapping variants. For clusters of more 442 

than two overlapping variants, this procedure is applied iteratively until no further overlaps 443 

remain. 444 

4. Long -clipping -associated regions. For any alignment containing a clipped segment ≥30 bp, the 445 

100 bp window flanking the clipped end is designated as a noisy region. For these regions, the 446 

putative variant size is fixed at V = 1.447 

Filtering of noisy regions. Identified noisy regions are filtered based on read -level support .448 

Regions are retained only if (i ) the total read coverage is ≥5; (ii ) the number of alternate reads , i.e., 449 

supporting a variant or containing clippings , is ≥2; (iii ) the ratio of alternate to total reads is ≥0.20. 450 

Merging of noisy regions. After filtering, each remaining noisy region is first extended to fully 451 

cover any overlapping low -complexity regions. LongcallD then merges two adjacent noisy regions 452 

i and j if the distance between their intervals is ≤ Vmin , where Vmin = min( Vi, Vj). The merged region 453 is assigned a putative variant size Vnew = max( Vi, Vj). This merging procedure is applied recursively 454 

until no additional regions satisfy the merging criterion . Finally, each remaining noisy region is 455 

extended by 10 bp on both flanks. 456 

Clean -region germline variant calling and read haplot agging 457 

Candidate variants located outside noisy regions are designated as clean -region variants. Variants 458 

with total coverage <5, alternate read coverage <2 or alternate allele ratio < 0.20 are filtered out. 459 

Variants with alternate allele ratios between 0.20 and 0.80 are classified as heterozygous, whereas 460 

those with ratios >0.80 are classified as homozygous. 461 

LongcallD phases variants and long reads in clean regions using an iterative haplotype 462 

extension strategy ( Fig. 1d ). A binary read –variant compatibility matrix is constructed, where 463 

entries indicate whether a read matches the reference allele (0), variant allele (1), or does not cover 464 

the site (-). Phasing is initialized from the heterozygous variant with the highest coverage, 465 

assigning the alternate and reference alleles to haplotypes 1 and 2, respectively. Reads covering 466 

this variant are assigned haplotypes accordingly. Phasing is then extended outward from the initial 467 

variant, first toward the left and subsequently toward the right along the genome .468 

Phasing proceeds iteratively by alternating between variant haplotype assignment and read 469 

haplotype assignment. Variants inherit haplotypes from overlapping reads by majority voting and 470 

will remain unassigned in the event of a tie . Conversely, reads inherit haplotypes from covered 471 

variants by majority voting and likewise remain unassigned if no majority exists . Iteration 472 

continues until haplotype assignments converge or until all reads and variants have been traversed 473 

ten times . Any two heterozygous variants covered by at least two shared reads are assigned to the 474 

same phase set. Each phase set is labeled by the smallest genomic coordinate among the variant s475 

it contains . Each read is assigned with the smallest phase set among all the variants it cover s.476 

Germline variant calling in noisy regions 477 

Haplotype -aware consensus calling. Reads overlapping a noisy region are classified as fully 478 

spanning if they cover both region boundaries , or partially spanning if they cover only one 479 

boundary. For each noisy region, longcallD searches for a phase set satisfying : (i) at least one fully 480 

spanning read per haplotype; and (ii) at least two total reads per haplotype . If multiple phase sets 481 

meet these criteria, the set with the largest number of reads is selected ; ties are resolved by 482 

choosing the left -most phase set .483 Sequences corresponding to the noisy region are extracted from both fully and partially 484 

spanning reads and aligned separately for each haplotype using abPOA 38 to generate haplotype -485 

specific consensus sequences. Sequences are incorporated into the partial order graph in 486 

descending order of length, with fully spanning sequences added prior to partially spanning 487 

sequences. In practice, partially spanning sequences are first aligned to the longest fully spanning 488 

sequence using WFA 39 ; the resulting alignment determines their start and end nodes in the graph, 489 

enabling end -to -end sequence -to -graph alignment .490 

Haplotype -agnostic consensus calling. For noisy regions lacking a valid phase set, regions with 491 

<5 fully spanning reads are skipped. Otherwise, haplotype -agnostic consensus calling is performed 492 

using fully spanning reads only. Sequences are sorted by length and aligned using abPOA, and the 493 

resulting partial order graph is converted into a row –column multiple sequence alignment. 494 

Alignment columns are designated as candidate heterozygous columns if two bases each 495 

have coverage ≥2 and ≤0.8× Cov total , where Cov total denotes the total column coverage. Columns 496 

are classified as substitutions when both bases are non -gaps and as indels when one base is a gap. 497 

Based on these columns, abPOA computes a pairwise distance matrix among sequences, weighting 498 

substitutions as 2 and indels as 1 . K -medoids clustering is applied to partition sequences into two 499 

clusters. Clustering is accepted if each cluster contains 20 –80% of sequences. If accepted, two 500 

haplotype -specific consensus sequences are generated; otherwise, a single consensus sequence is 501 

produced, indicating a homozygous noisy region. 502 

Variant calling from haplotype consensus sequences. Consensus sequences are aligned to the 503 

reference sequence of the noisy region using WFA. For homozygous noisy regions, variants are 504 

called directly from the alignment. For heterozygous regions, variants are called independently 505 

from each haplotype consensus sequence. Variants present in both haplotypes are reported as 506 

homozygous, whereas haplotype -specific variants are reported as heterozygous. 507 

Haplotype phasing in noisy regions . After variant calling across all noisy regions, longcallD 508 

performs a second round of haplotype phasing jointly across clean -region and noisy -region 509 

variants. The procedure mirrors clean -region phasing, except that reads inherit haplotypes from 510 

covered variants using weighted majority voting: clean -region variants are assigned weight 2 and 511 

noisy -region variants weight 1. For noisy -region variants, the read –variant compatibility matrix is 512 

constructed by aligning haplotype consensus sequences to individual reads to determine allele 513 

support. 514 Low -VAF mosaic variant calling 515 

LongcallD performs additional low -VAF mosaic variant calling after germline variant calling. 516 

Currently, mosaic SN Vs and large indels are supported, whereas mosaic small indels are not called 517 

due to the difficulty of distinguishing them from long -read sequencing errors. 518 

Collecting candidate mosaic variants. During germline variant calling, filtered SNPs and large 519 

indels (length ≥30 bp ) with alternate allele ratio <0.20 and alternate read coverage ≥2 are recorded 520 

as candidate mosaic variants. Candidate mosaic MEIs are identified by requiring: (i) the presence 521 

of TSD ≥2 bp and poly(A) tail sequences ≥10 bp within large insertions; and (ii) the inserted 522 

sequence contains ≥3 non -overlapping 15 -bp subsequences of any known mobile elements (e.g., 523 

Alu, L1, and SVA). For mosaic MEIs, the alternate read coverage threshold is reduced to one .524 

Selection of phase set. For each candidate mosaic variant, longcallD collects all reference and 525 

alternate reads covering the variant and identifies a phase set satisfying the following criteria: (i) 526 

it contains the largest number of alternate reads among all phase sets; (ii) all alternate reads 527 

originate from the same haplotype; (iii) the number of alternate reads does not exceed the number 528 

of reference reads from the same haplotype and phase set; and (iv) the total number of reference 529 

and alternate reads is ≥5. Once a phase set was selected, only alternate reads from this phase set 530 

will be used in the following filtering step. 531 

Filtering candidate mosaic variants. Candidate mosaic SN Vs are filtered out if any of the 532 

following conditions are met: (i) the distance to any germline variant is <5 bp; (ii) the median base 533 

quality of alternate bases is lower than the median base quality of all bases; (iii) the median of the 534 

minimum base quality scores within 7 -bp windows (variant base ± 3 bp) around alternate bases is 535 

lower than the first quartile base quality of all bases; (iv) the median distance between alternate 536 

bases and the nearest indel sequencing error is <5 bp; (v) ≥50% of alternate bases lie within 500 537 

bp of a dense -difference window (50 bp window containing >5 differing bases); (vi) ≥ 50% of 538 

alternate bases lie within 100 bp of a clipping event with length ≥100 bp; or (vii) ≥ 50% of alternate 539 

reads contain sequencing errors within LCRs covering the variant. 540 

Candidate mosaic large indels are filtered out if any of the following conditions are met: 541 

(i) the variant lies within 500 bp of a germline indel and either ≥80% of the differing sequence is 542 

low -complexity or the sequence similarity between the two variants is ≥80%; (ii) the alternate read 543 

count equals 1 and ≥80% of the variant sequence is low -complexity; (iii) ≥50% of alternate bases 544 lie within 500 bp of a dense -difference window (50 bp window containing >5 differing bases); or 545 

(iv) ≥50% of alternate bases lie within 100 bp of a clipping event with length ≥100 bp. 546 

Post -filtering of mosaic variants. Candidate mosaic variants located within 1 kb windows 547 

containing more than five mosaic variants are removed. supporting filtered mosaic variants are 548 

marked as invalid for mosaic calling. Remaining mosaic variants supported by any invalid reads 549 

are subsequently filtered out. 550 

ONT -specific processing 551 

Due to the higher error rate and increased prevalence of artifacts in ONT reads compared with 552 

PacBio HiFi reads, longcallD applies additional ONT -specific filters. 553 

Palindromic clippings. If a supplementary alignment indicates that a clipped sequence maps to 554 

the same locus as the primary alignment and the overlapping region covers ≥ 90% of the primary 555 

alignment, the clipping is classified as palindromic and is not treated as a noisy region. 556 

Stranded bias. For each candidate germline variant, longcallD performs a Fisher’s exact test 557 

comparing the observed numbers of forward - and reverse -strand alternate reads with the expected 558 

counts (assumed equal). Variants with P < 0.01 are classified as strand -biased and filtered out. For 559 

mosaic variant calling , in addition to the criteria above, candidate mosaic SN Vs are filtered out if 560 

all alternate reads originate exclusively from either the forward or the reverse strand. 561 

Harmonizing somatic SV call set as ground -truth mosaic variants 562 

A d e novo assembly of HiFi reads from the H2009 normal cell line was generated using hifiasm 563 

(v0.19.6) . HiFi long reads from the H2009 tumor sample were aligned to four references: GRCh38, 564 

CHM13v2 linear genome 40 , and the normal -sample de novo assembl y using minimap2 (v2.28) 41 ,565 

and to CHM13 graph genome 42 using minigraph (v0.21) 43 . Small variants in the normal cell line 566 

were called from long -read data using Clair3 7 with phasing enabled. The resulting variant calls 567 

and read alignments were provided to WhatsHap 44 to generate the phased alignment file s.568 

We applied minisv (v0.1.2) 36 in tumor –normal paired mode, integrating the tumor 569 

alignments described above together with GRCh38 -based alignments of the normal sample to 570 

identify somatic SVs, with coordinates reported on GRCh38 . An initial GRCh38 -based SV call set 571 

was generated using parameters “-c 1 -s 0 ” to retain a comprehensive set of candidates where all 572 

calls have at least one supporting read, which was used for mosaic MEI calling evaluation . For 573 

downstream consensus harmonization, a more stringent cutoff , i.e., “-c 2 -s 0 ” was applied to only 574 

retain calls with at least two supporting reads .575 In parallel, somatic SVs were identified on GRCh38 using tumor –normal paired mode in 576 

Severus (v1.0) 18 and SAVANA (v1.2) 34 with the phased alignment file, enabling VNTR -aware 577 

mode under default settings . Nanomonsv (v0.7) 35 was applied to the original alignment file to 578 

generate GRCh38 -based somatic SV calls. For all methods, read ID output was enabled. Each SV 579 

call set, together with its corresponding read ID file, was processed using minisv filterasm with 580 

breakpoint signals derived from de novo assembly –based read alignments. This step restricted 581 

calls to high -confidence regions (https://github.com/lh3/minisv/blob/master/data/hs38.reg.bed), 582 

excluding pseudo -autosomal regions on chromosomes X and Y, centromeric regions, and 583 

acrocentric short arms . Finally, all filtered SV call sets were harmonized using the minisv union 584 

with parameters “ -p -c 5 ”, which identifies consensus SVs across methods and retains calls 585 

supported by at least five reads. The resulting consensus set was used as the reference mosaic SV 586 

call set for evaluation .587
