# CLOP-DiT: Structured-Metadata-Conditioned Single-Cell Latent Generation via Contrastive Language-Omics Pretraining and Diffusion Transformers 

Zeyu Fu 

State Key Laboratory of Trauma and Chemical Poisoning, Institute of Combined Injury, Chongqing Engineering Research Center for Nanomedicine, College of Preventive Medicine, Army Medical University, Chongqing 400038, China 

Simple Summary 1

CLOP-DiT is a computational pipeline that generates synthetic single-cell gene expression 2

profiles from structured biological descriptions (cell type, tissue, organism, marker genes, 3

and disease context). It first learns to align text descriptions with real cell data in a shared 4

mathematical space, then uses a diffusion model to generate new cell states matching a 5

given description. The generated cells capture correct cell-type identity and marker gene 6

patterns, but do not yet reproduce the full cell-to-cell variability seen in real single-cell 7

experiments. This work demonstrates that text-guided single-cell generation is feasible as a 8

proof of concept, opening directions for future simulation and hypothesis-generation tools 9

in biology. 10 

Abstract 11 

Generating realistic single-cell transcriptomic profiles from structured biological descrip- 12 

tions would enable controlled simulation, data augmentation, and hypothesis-driven 13 

cell-state creation—yet no existing method combines text–cell alignment with conditional 14 

generation. We present CLOP-DiT, a modular three-stage pipeline: (1) a contrastive aligner 15 

(CLOP) maps BiomedBERT text embeddings and scGPT cell embeddings into a shared 16 

512-dimensional space; (2) a conditional Diffusion Transformer (DiT) generates scGPT- 17 

compatible latent states via flow matching, steered by a five-field biological template (cell 18 

type, tissue, organism, marker genes, disease); and (3) a frozen scGPT decoder maps latents 19 

to gene expression. Across 69 cell types from 80 GEO datasets (220,304 cells), a high-fidelity 20 

regime (CFG = 2.0) achieves 36.9% KNN accuracy (25 × chance) and 81.0% steering, while 21 

a high-diversity regime (CFG = 1.0) reaches diversity ratio 0.93 at 80.7% steering. Condi- 22 

tioning field ablation and swap-label permutation tests confirm that marker genes are the 23 

dominant steering signal (steering accuracy drops from 99.8% to 62.4% when only metadata 24 

fields are retained). Key limitations are identified transparently: in-distribution per-gene 25 

variance structure is well preserved ( r = 0.98) but cross-dataset variance correlation drops 26 

to near zero, the discriminator AUC of 0.656 indicates residual distinguishability, and a 27 

pilot rare-cell augmentation study was negative. The modular architecture enables targeted 28 

remediation of each limitation without full retraining. CLOP-DiT establishes the feasibil- 29 

ity of structured-metadata-conditioned single-cell generation and provides a composable 30 

framework for iterative improvement. 31

1. Introduction 35 

Single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) has transformed the study of cellular het- 36 

erogeneity by enabling transcriptome-scale profiling at single-cell resolution [ 1, 2]. Yet 37 

generating synthetic single-cell states from structured biological descriptors remains dif- 38 

ficult. A generative model conditioned on cell identity, tissue context, organism, marker 39 

genes, and disease state is attractive for controlled simulation and hypothesis generation, 40 

and its value depends on whether it preserves biologically meaningful structure rather 41 

than merely the fluency of the textual prompt. 42 

Most current single-cell generative models condition on categorical labels or per- 43 

turbation metadata rather than richer multi-field descriptions. Variational autoencoders 44 

such as scVI [ 3,4] and scGen [ 5], geometric generative models [ 6 ], and foundation models 45 

such as Geneformer [ 7] and scBERT [ 8–11 ] broaden the modeling landscape, but they do 46 

not jointly learn a structured text–cell alignment space and a conditional diffusion-style 47 

generator. Cell2Sentence [ 12 ] and GenePT [ 13] move closer to language-based biology, yet 48 

they address different problem formulations and do not combine contrastive cross-modal 49 

alignment with latent flow matching. 50 

The most closely related concurrent work is CellWhisperer [ 42 ], which also bridges 51 

natural language and single-cell transcriptomics via contrastive alignment. However, 52 

CellWhisperer is fundamentally discriminative : it retrieves and annotates existing cells by 53 

matching text queries to a reference atlas, but it does not generate novel single-cell expres- 54 

sion profiles. CLOP-DiT occupies the complementary generative niche—given a structured 55 

biological description, it synthesises new cell states that do not exist in the training data. 56 

This distinction is important because controlled generation enables applications such as 57 

data augmentation for rare cell types, in-silico perturbation simulation, and hypothesis- 58 

driven creation of cell states not yet experimentally observed. Table 1 summarizes the key 59 

differences. 60

Recent advances in contrastive multimodal learning and diffusion modeling motivate 61 

a direct bridge between biological descriptions and cell representations. CLIP/SigLIP-style 62 

objectives show that strong cross-modal alignment can emerge from contrastive train- 63 

ing [ 14 ,15 ], while Diffusion Transformers and flow matching provide scalable conditional 64 

generation in continuous latent spaces [ 16– 19 ]. These ingredients motivate the design of 65 

CLOP-DiT: first build a well-separated text–cell condition space, then learn a conditional 66 

sampler in a biologically meaningful latent representation. 67 

We present CLOP-DiT, a three-stage pipeline that aligns structured metadata with 68 

scGPT cell embeddings, samples scGPT latents conditioned on that aligned representation, 69 

and decodes latents back to gene expression. The input is a fixed five-field template—cell 70 

type, tissue, organism, marker genes, and disease context—and the method therefore con- 71

The main contributions are fourfold. First, CLOP uses PrototypeSigLIP with ZCA- 76 

whitened BiomedBERT embeddings to align text and cell representations in a shared 77 

512-dimensional space and improve inter-type separability by ∼130 × (pairwise cosine 78 

similarity from 0.994 to 0.222). Second, a 1D Diffusion Transformer performs conditional 79 

flow matching in that latent space to generate type-specific cell embeddings. Third, we eval- 80 

uate the model on 69 deduplicated cell types from 80 GEO datasets using complementary 81 

latent, diversity, and downstream biological metrics. Fourth, we report both the strengths 82 

and the limits of the approach: structured conditioning produces clear above-chance type 83 

specificity, but variance preservation, covariance preservation, and rare-cell augmentation 84 

remain unresolved. 85 

Across the main operating points, CLOP-DiT reaches 36.9% KNN accuracy and 81.0% 86 

steering in a high-fidelity regime, while a high-diversity regime achieves a diversity ra- 87 

tio of 0.93 at 80.7% steering. Multi-seed replication shows stable steering and diversity, 88 

whereas decoder-level analyses reveal strong mean reconstruction but weak preservation 89 

of within-population heterogeneity. CLOP-DiT is a proof-of-concept structured-metadata- 90 

to-latent conditional sampler with a clear roadmap for improvement, not a finished tool for 91 

downstream biological analysis. 92 

2. Materials and Methods 93 

Figure 1 provides an overview of the pipeline. We organize the Materials and Meth- 94

and training—covering CLOP alignment (2.2), DiT generation (2.3), and scGPT decoding 96

2.1. Dataset 99 

We curated 80 datasets from the Gene Expression Omnibus (GEO) spanning cancer and 100 

developmental biology, comprising 220,304 cells after quality control and highly variable 101 

gene selection [ 20, 21 ] (2,000 highly variable genes per dataset, selected by mean expression 102 

and dispersion). Each cell was encoded into a 512-dimensional embedding by a pre-trained 103 

scGPT model. Cell-type annotations were organized into 1,088 unique text groups, each 104 

described by a structured text caption (not free-form natural language) incorporating cell 105 

type, marker genes, tissue of origin, organism, and disease context. Text descriptions were 106 

generated for each cell type using a structured template: 107 

{cell type}, tissue: {tissue}, organism: {organism}, 108 

markers: {top 5 DE marker genes}, context: {disease/condition} 109 

where marker genes were identified per cell type by one-vs.-rest Wilcoxon rank-sum 110 

test [ 22 ] on the training set. As an independent check on marker gene circularity, we vali- 111 

dated these training-set-derived markers against PanglaoDB [ 23 ] and CellMarker 2.0 [ 24]; 112 

the Jaccard overlap (0.39–0.45, well above the ∼0.01 expected for random gene sets of this 113 

size) indicates that the selected markers converge with biological consensus (see Discussion). 114 

The exact template and marker gene sources are available in the project repository [45]. 115

2.2. CLOP: Contrastive Language–Omics Pretraining 160 

CLOP aligns structured metadata descriptions and cell profiles into a shared 512- 161 

dimensional embedding space using a PrototypeSigLIP contrastive loss, producing the 162 

well-separated condition space required by the DiT. A frozen BiomedBERT-large encoder 163 

maps text descriptions to 1024-dimensional embeddings, and a frozen scGPT encoder 164 

maps cells to 512-dimensional embeddings. Dual three-layer MLP projectors (text: 1024 → 165 

1024 → 1024 → 512; cell: 512 → 512 → 512 → 512) with BatchNorm [ 27 ], GELU 166 

activation [ 28 ], and dropout [ 29 ] (0.2) project both modalities onto the L2-normalized unit 167 

hypersphere. 168 

ZCA whitening. 169 

Prior to projection, Zero-phase Component Analysis (ZCA) whitening is applied to the 170 

BiomedBERT embeddings to decorrelate dimensions and mitigate the collapse typical of 171 

frozen BERT representations. The transform is fitted once on the training text embeddings 172 

using the full 1024 × 1024 covariance matrix with regularisation ϵ = 10 −4, then reused 173 

unchanged for validation and inference text. All components are retained; the goal is 174 

decorrelation rather than dimensionality reduction. In preliminary sensitivity checks, ZCA 175 

produced substantially lower pairwise cosine similarity than mean-centering or LayerNorm, 176 

and it was therefore adopted as the production preprocessing step. 177

2.3. DiT: Conditional Flow Matching with Diffusion Transformer 205 

The Diffusion Transformer (DiT1D) processes cell embeddings as sequences of 16 206 

pseudo-tokens, each 32-dimensional (totaling 512-d). The architecture comprises 8 AdaLN- 207 

Zero transformer blocks with 8-head self-attention (head dimension 64) and feed-forward 208 

layers (512 → 2048 → 512). Timestep information is encoded via sinusoidal embed- 209 

dings projected to 512 dimensions, and CLOP condition vectors are projected from 512 210 

to 512 dimensions. The combined condition ccombined = temb + cemb (element-wise sum- 211 

mation, not concatenation) modulates each block through 6 AdaLN-Zero parameters 212 

(γ1, β1, α1, γ2, β2, α2), all zero-initialized for stable early training. The model has 22.10M 213 

parameters (Table A1). 214

2.4. Decoding via scGPT 235 

Generated latent vectors z1 ∈ R512 are mapped back to gene expression using the 236 

frozen scGPT decoder. This step is useful for downstream biological inspection, but it 237 

introduces an important limitation: the decoder is many-to-one, so diverse latent vectors 238 

near the training manifold can decode to similar expression profiles. We therefore treat 239 

latent-space metrics—KNN accuracy, steering, diversity ratio, and centroid cosine—as 240 

the primary indicators of generative quality, whereas expression-space analyses serve as 241 

decoder-induced compatibility checks rather than direct measures of generation fidelity. 242 

Breaking this bottleneck requires a trainable decoder or lightweight adapter, which is left 243 

to future work. 244 

2.5. Evaluation Framework 245 

We evaluate CLOP-DiT along six axes using in-distribution conditions (the actual 246 

CLOP-projected text embeddings from training data): 247 

1. KNN classification accuracy : For each generated cell, a k-nearest-neighbor classifier 248 

(k=15, cosine metric, PCA-50 features) trained on 80% of real data predicts its cell 249 

type. PCA is fitted on the real training split only and applied identically to generated 250 

embeddings; the same PCA transform is used across all method comparisons. Random 251 

chance is 1 / 69 ≈ 1.45%. Because real cell-type centroids in scGPT space have pairwise 252 

cosine similarity of 0.994, small embedding perturbations can substantially affect 253 

KNN accuracy; the biological meaning of absolute KNN values therefore warrants 254 

caution. 255 

2. Steering accuracy : For random cell-type pairs (A, B), we generate cells conditioned 256 

on A and test whether the generated centroid is closer to the real cluster A than B in 257 

centered space. Random chance is 50%. Note: At intermediate-to-high CFG values 258 

(e.g., CFG ≥ 1.5) with multi-step solvers, steering accuracy can saturate at 1.0 with 259 

bootstrap CI [1.0, 1.0], indicating a ceiling effect; this metric loses discriminative power 260 

once guidance is sufficient. 261 

3. Diversity ratio : Ratio of generated within-group variance to real within-group vari- 262 

ance. Ideal is 1.0; values <1 indicate mode sharpening; values >1 indicate excess 263 

noise. 264 

4. Coverage and density : Coverage measures the fraction of real samples that have at 265 

least one generated neighbor within the real data’s KNN radius ( k=5); it quantifies 266 

mode coverage. Density measures the average number of generated samples falling 267 

within each real sample’s KNN radius, normalised by k; it quantifies precision. Both 268 

are computed in PCA-50 embedding space. 269

Caveat on Fréchet Distance (FD). FD [ 38 ] compares distributional moments (mean 284 

and covariance) between real and generated samples. While widely used for unconditional 285 

evaluation, FD can be misleading for conditional generation: a model that reproduces the 286 

global marginal distribution but ignores conditioning achieves a low FD without having 287 

learned the target conditional. FD is therefore reported as a secondary metric throughout; 288 

the primary metrics (KNN accuracy, steering, diversity ratio) are specifically designed for 289 

conditional evaluation. 290

vs. 512 vs. 768) have minimal impact (within ±0.3 pp), indicating that the CLOP aligner is 548 

not sensitive to projection dimension in this range. Fixed temperature ( −4.61 pp) and high 549 

variant probability ( −6.03 pp) degrade performance substantially relative to the ablation 550 

baseline (which used a learnable temperature). Clarification: The ablation baseline used a 551 

learnable τ, whereas the final production model uses fixed τ = 14.0 in combination with a 552 

stratified validation split (Section 2.1). The production configuration was selected based on 553 

the full-pipeline generation quality assessment, not solely on CLOP prototype accuracy; 554 

the ablation table characterizes the CLOP alignment stage in isolation and does not directly 555 

predict downstream DiT generation performance. 556 

3.11. Rare Cell Augmentation 557 

As a proof-of-concept, we evaluated whether generated cells can improve classifier per- 558 

formance on an underrepresented type (“Cycling cells”, <5% prevalence). Augmentation 559 

at 1–10 × ratios did not improve Cycling-cell F1 (baseline 0.828 → 0.741–0.786 after augmen- 560 

tation), though overall macro F1 remained stable (0.947–0.964), indicating that synthetic 561 

profiles are realistic enough to avoid catastrophic interference but lack the within-type 562 

heterogeneity needed for net positive augmentation. 563

4. Discussion 594 

CLOP-DiT demonstrates that structured biological metadata drives non-trivial single- 595 

cell latent generation, but the method operates within a narrow and precisely defined scope. 596 

The conditioning signal is a fixed five-field template rather than free-form language, and 597 

the model is a structured-metadata-to-latent sampler, not a general biological language 598 

model. Within that scope, the central result is clear: conditioning produces above-chance, 599 

cell-type-specific latent generation and supports two useful operating regimes that trade 600 

off fidelity against diversity. 601 

Variance and covariance deficits. The strongest biological limitation is the loss of 602 

cell-to-cell heterogeneity. Although in-distribution per-gene variance is well correlated 603 

(r = 0.98, Figure A1), cross-dataset variance ratios fall orders of magnitude below unity 604 

(Figure A1d), and within-type gene–gene correlation structure is only weakly preserved. 605 

A pilot latent-space analysis (Figure 9, panels a–d) shows that generated latents preserve 606 

overall spread better than per-dimension variance structure, while Mantel correlations 607 

of within-type gene–gene matrices remain weak (Figure 9, panels e–h). Both findings 608 

point to the same root cause: the flow-matching objective matches conditional means far 609 

better than second-order structure. This is addressable within the current architecture via 610 

a variance-matching regularization term (Equation 4) added to the DiT training objective 611 

alone; early experiments at λ = 0.1 show stable training dynamics. 612 

Extended validation. Five additional experiments characterize the model beyond 613 

core metrics; full figures are provided in Appendix A.1. Cross-dataset gene-level valida- 614 

tion across five tissues (Figure A1) confirms high mean-expression correlation ( r > 0.999) 615 

but near-zero variance correlation, reinforcing that mean-level fidelity does not extend 616 

to distributional agreement. Expanded DE concordance across five contrasts (Figure A1) 617 

yields log-fold-change correlations from r = 0.17 to r = 0.58 with >85% sign agreement. 618 

OOD evaluation of six novel cell types and eight free-form prompts (Figures A1 and A1) 619 

shows limited marker recovery due to vocabulary constraints but maintained embedding 620 

coherence. Marker gene program completeness (Figure A1) varies from near-zero to 0.60 621 

recall@100 across cell types. Embedding-level augmentation of rare cell types (Figure A1) 622 

yields marginal gains ( ∆F1 = + 0.001), confirming mean-collapse as the primary augmenta- 623 

tion bottleneck. A validation synthesis (Figure A1) summarizes these findings. 624 

Ablation, reproducibility, and decoder analysis. The CLOP ablation heatmap (Fig- 625 

ure A1) consolidates 15 experiments, confirming cohesion and noise regularization as the 626 

most impactful components (Table 5). Multi-seed validation (Figure A1) shows stable 627 

steering ( σ = 0.009) and diversity ( σ ≤ 0.016) across three independent runs (Table 3). 628

Benchmarking and operating regimes. On the primary common-metrics-only com- 635 

parison, the Gaussian baseline outperforms CLOP-DiT, underscoring that mean-matching 636 

remains competitive when evaluation ignores conditioning-sensitive biology. The more 637 

informative result is that CLOP-DiT creates a controllable fidelity–diversity trade-off: CFG 638 

= 2.0 with Euler sampling yields stronger type fidelity, whereas CFG = 1.0 with Midpoint 639 

sampling preserves substantially more diversity. This trade-off does not erase the remaining 640 

gap to real data (KNN 36.9%, discriminator AUC 0.656). 641 

Limitations and scope. The 36.9% KNN accuracy implies an approximate 63% label- 642 

noise rate; applications requiring high type-label confidence need post-hoc filtering. The 643 

high-fidelity regime (DivR = 0.513) collapses toward centroids, erasing within-population 644 

heterogeneity needed for trajectory inference or sub-clustering; the high-diversity regime 645 

(DivR = 0.93) mitigates this at reduced type specificity (KNN ≈ 29%). The pilot rare-cell 646 

augmentation was negative at both expression and embedding levels, consistent with mean- 647 

collapse: generated cells cluster near type centroids rather than spanning the full within- 648 

type distribution. All results are restricted to human and mouse cancer/developmental 649 

datasets; comparison with CellWhisperer [42] and Cell2Sentence remains future work. 650 

Modular architecture as a path forward. CLOP-DiT’s three-stage design enables 651 

targeted improvements without full retraining: a variance-matching penalty (Equation 4) 652 

can be added to the DiT objective alone; decoder fine-tuning via LoRA adapters can relieve 653 

the frozen-decoder bottleneck; and richer prompt representations can be incorporated 654 

by retraining only the CLOP aligner. This composability distinguishes CLOP-DiT from 655 

joint-training approaches [43] and positions it as a framework for iterative improvement. 656 

5. Conclusions 657 

We introduced CLOP-DiT, a three-stage pipeline for structured-metadata-conditioned 658 

single-cell latent generation. CLOP aligns BiomedBERT text embeddings and scGPT cell 659 

embeddings in a shared 512-dimensional space, and a conditional Diffusion Transformer 660 

then samples scGPT latents from a fixed five-field biological template. The method is a 661 

structured-metadata-to-latent conditional sampler, not a free-form language model. Across 662 

69 deduplicated cell types from 80 datasets, CLOP-DiT achieves 36.9% KNN accuracy and 663 

81.0% steering in a high-fidelity regime, while a high-diversity regime reaches a diversity 664 

ratio of 0.93 at 80.7% steering. Multi-seed replication confirms that steering and diversity are 665 

stable across independent runs. A conditioning field ablation and swap-label permutation 666 

test provide causal evidence that the conditioning signal is semantically functional. Five 667 

downstream experiments—cross-dataset biological validation, expanded DE concordance, 668 

OOD robustness, marker gene program completeness, and embedding-level augmen- 669 

tation—collectively support the model’s specificity and highlight mean-collapse as the 670 

primary practical limitation (Figure A1). 671 

Scope of claims. All results are limited to in-distribution human and mouse can- 672 

cer and developmental datasets; no stringent out-of-distribution generalization test has 673 

been conducted. Claims about generative quality refer primarily to latent-space metrics; 674 

expression-level concordance is strongly shaped by the frozen scGPT decoder and up- 675 

stream embedding preprocessing and therefore does not constitute evidence of faithful 676 

transcriptomic generation. 677
