## 摘要
mRNA 丰度表型的遗传关联研究将调控基因位点与 mRNA 丰度（数量性状位点；QTL）联系起来。大多数 eQTL 研究局限于基因水平的 mRNA，而未关注 mRNA 异构体。在本研究中，我们利用一个大规模、遗传多样的小鼠群体，对基因和转录本异构体丰度的 QTL 进行了定位。我们鉴定出高度重叠的局部调控 mRNA 集合，这与主要不重叠的远端调控 mRNA 形成对比，特别是那些受性别和饮食影响的 mRNA。利用蛋白质编码基因-异构体对的局部 QTL 等位基因效应模式，我们证明遗传变异驱动了等位基因特异性的异构体使用，通过主要的转录后机制产生其遗传信号与其聚合基因水平效应相背离的异构体。我们对远端 mRNA 热点进行了通路富集分析，发现了基因水平性状未能检测到的异构体水平通路。随后，我们在远端热点应用了一种复合中介方法，比较了基因-基因、异构体-异构体以及异构体-基因的中介模型。通过对比这些转录调控的因果模型，我们鉴定出了在基因水平上未被发现的 mRNA 异构体之间的独特关联。我们还表征了性别和饮食对 mRNA 表达的影响。我们的数据还表明，性别和饮食主要通过远端作用的基因位点影响表达。我们将 QTL 数据与人类遗传数据相结合，优先筛选了与代谢相关性状关联位点中的效应基因，这些基因暗示了人类中对性别和饮食的条件依赖性。总之，我们的研究结果突出了转录调控的独特机制，并强调在遗传关联研究中优先关注异构体水平的必要性，以避免仅进行基因水平 QTL 定位可能导致的信号遗漏。

## Abstract
Genetic association studies of mRNA abundance phenotypes link regulatory gene loci to mRNA abundance (quantitative trait loci; QTL). The majority of eQTL studies are limited to gene-level mRNAs and have not focused on mRNA isoforms. Here, we utilized a large, genetically diverse mouse population to map QTL for both gene and transcript isoform abundance. We identified largely overlapping sets of locally regulated mRNAs, which contrasted with the predominantly non-overlapping distally regulated mRNAs, particularly those influenced by sex and diet. Using allele-effect patterns from local QTL for protein-coding gene-isoform pairs, we show that genetic variation drives allele-specific isoform usage, generating isoforms whose genetic signals diverge from their aggregated gene-level effects through predominantly post-transcriptional mechanisms. We conducted pathway enrichment on distal mRNA hotspots and uncovered isoform-level pathways not detected with gene-level traits. We then applied a composite mediation approach at distal hotspots that compares gene-gene, isoform-isoform, and isoform-gene mediator models. By contrasting these causal models of transcriptional regulation, we identified unique associations between mRNA isoforms that were undetected at the gene level. We also characterized the influence of sex and diet on mRNA expression. Our data also suggest that sex and diet influence expression primarily through distal-acting gene loci. We integrated our QTL data with human genetic data, prioritizing effector genes in loci associated with metabolically relevant traits that suggest conditional dependence on sex and diet in humans. Overall, our findings highlight distinctive mechanisms of transcriptional regulation and emphasize the need to prioritize an isoform-level focus for genetic association studies to avoid missed signals that may arise from the gene-level only QTL mapping.
