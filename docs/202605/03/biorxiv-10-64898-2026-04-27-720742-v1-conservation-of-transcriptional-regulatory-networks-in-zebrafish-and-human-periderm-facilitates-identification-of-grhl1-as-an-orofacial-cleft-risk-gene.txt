Abstract 24 

Most heritable risk for orofacial clefts (OFC) remains unassigned to specific genes or 25 

loci. IRF6 and GRHL3 , two established OFC risk genes, encode transcription factors 26 

(TFs) essential for the differentiation of the periderm, a transient embryonic tissue 27 

required for secondary palate fusion. To identify novel risk candidates, we modeled the 28 

zebrafish periderm transcriptional regulatory network (TRN). Using single-cell multiome 29 

sequencing (RNA-seq and ATAC-seq) from shield-stage embryos, we inferred TF-to-30 

target gene connections by integrating correlated gene expression with TF binding site 31 

predictions within chromatin elements open in periderm cells. We generated sets of 32 

gold-standard edges by conducting RNA-seq on TF-depleted embryos and ChIP-33 

seq/CUT&RUN on wild-type embryos and used them to benchmark model performance. 34 

Within the top-performing model, zebrafish periderm modules are strongly preserved in 35 

human embryonic periderm and orthologs of human OFC-associated genes have higher 36 

centrality and edge-sum scores than non-associated genes. Functional validation 37 

confirmed the network's predictive power: depleting high-centrality TFs, including grhl1 ,38 

klf6a , tead3b , and klf17 , disrupted periderm differentiation in sensitized embryos. 39 

Moreover, analysis of whole-genome sequencing data from 2,415 OFC trios identified 40 

15 individuals with rare or de novo GRHL1 variants, four of which introduced premature 41 

stop codons. This study establishes GRHL1 as a novel OFC risk gene and highlights 42 

the power of cross-species gene regulatory network analysis to prioritize candidates for 43 

rare variants in complex structural birth defects. 44

Introduction 47 

The role of rare genetic variants in pathogenesis of orofacial cleft (OFC), a congenital 48 

birth defect which comprises cleft lip, cleft palate, or a combination of the two, is 49 

incompletely understood. In most of the cases, OFC occurs as the only anomaly and is 50 

termed non-syndromic (nsOFC) 1. Evidence for a genetic contribution to the etiology of 51 

nsOFC is that the concordance of nsOFC in monozygotic twins (about 50%) is higher 52 

than that in dizygotic twins (about 5%) 2,3 . Multiple genome wide association studies 53 

(GWAS) and meta-analyses of them have identified more than 40 loci where common 54 

variants are associated with risk for OFC 4-10 . There is debate regarding how to 55 

accurately infer the fraction of overall heritability of complex diseases 11 . However, most 56 

GWAS have limited power to evaluate polymorphisms with minor allele frequencies 57 

under 5% 12 and it is well accepted that common variants contribute only a fraction of 58 

the heritable risk for nsOFC 13,14 . Rarer variants can be identified through exome or 59 

whole genome sequencing of individuals with OFCs, but such efforts identify thousands 60 

of rare variants in every individual, not just those with a birth anomaly 15-17 . Moreover, all 61 

individuals harbor many variants that computer algorithms predict alter protein function 62 

and many more that have unknown functional significance 18 . It is thus an outstanding 63 

challenge in the field to identify the rare variants that contribute risk for OFC. 64 

To prioritize genes for their likelihood of harboring variants that will cause OFC 65 

pathogenesis it is helpful to know the genes that govern differentiation of craniofacial 66 

tissues. Among these tissues is periderm, a simple squamous epithelium present in 67 

embryos prior to the maturation of epidermis that serves as barrier to dehydration 19 . In 68 

murine embryos periderm is generated after stratification of early embryonic epidermis 69

To identify novel regulators of EVL differentiation we sought to construct models of the 97 

EVL transcriptional regulatory network (TRN). TRNs are comprised of the set of 98 

transcription factors (TFs) expressed in a given tissue and their regulatory connections 99 

(edges) to target genes; they are a reductionist description of the genetic circuitry that 100 

governs cellular differentiation 39-41 . TRNs are cumbersome to deduce experimentally 101 

but can be inferred computationally 42 . TRN-predicting algorithms infer edges between 102 

genes if their expression is correlated in multiple gene expression profiles (gene 103 

expression co-variation). Single cell RNA sequencing experiments, which can yield 104 

hundreds of profiles for a given cell type, are a useful source for these datasets, with 105 

some caveats, including dropouts 43 . Some algorithms additionally infer edges between 106 

TFs and target genes if the canonical TF binding site (TFBS) is present in open 107 

chromatin near the transcriptional start site of the target gene; we will refer to this type 108 

of evidence as “prior” following the nomenclature of the study that inspired this one 44 .109 

Single cell ATAC seq data can be useful in this context as they can reveal cell-type 110 

specific open chromatin elements; however, they require large numbers of cells per 111 

cluster to overcome the sparseness inherent in there being just two copies of each 112 

genomic locus in every cell 45 .113

Network inference 160 

A list target genes TRN inference was prepared comprising of 3,787 genes that were 161 

expressed in 10 percent or more of the EVL cells regardless of their relative expression 162 

in EVL cells versus in other cell types. Gene expression matrix was prepared by taking 163 

the FPKM values from gene expression (in rows) data of 394 EVL cells (in columns). 164 

Very low expressing genes (expressed in less than 10 percent cells) were excluded 165 

from the matrix. 166 

Gene expression and prior information matrices were used to infer TRN models using 167 

four different algorithms: WGCNA 49 , GENIE3 50 , gLASSO (graphical lasso) 51 , and 168 

Inferelator 3.0 52 . All four algorithms produce a score per edge (correlation, regression 169 

coefficient, etc.) that reflects the strength of evidence for that edge. For WGCNA and 170 

GENIE3.0, these scores were upweighted by a factor that depends on the prior strength 171 

(0 meaning no change, 1 meaning that the score is doubled, and so on). For gLASSO 172 

and INFERELATOR, the strength was directly represented by a penalty or prior, 173 

respectively. 174

Applying a threshold to each algorithm’s scores, the edge predictions can be compared 226 

to the ground truth to obtain precision (percent of edges above threshold that are true 227 

positives) and recall (percent of true positives above threshold). Varying the threshold 228 

produces a precision-recall curve. We used eigenvector centrality, which 229 

accommodates continuous edge scores, to quantify the importance of TFs as central 230 

players in the network. For non-TF genes, which always have low centrality, we 231 

quantified their importance by the weighted sum of edges pointing to it across all TFs 232 

(“EdgeSum”). The Walktrap community detection algorithm 60 was used to cluster TFs in 233 

the network into modules. 234

these tuning parameters yielded more than 50 models of the EVL TRN ( Tables S9 – 352

Discussion 522 

In this study, we employed a multi-modal systems biology approach to bridge the gap 523 

between periderm development and the genetic architecture of OFC condition. By 524 

integrating single-cell multiome (ATAC + RNA-seq) with computational inference of 525 

network models, we identified a core transcriptional regulatory network (TRN) governing 526 

periderm differentiation in zebrafish. A key strength of this study was the functional 527 

validation of network hubs. While single-gene knockdowns of high-centrality TFs did not 528 

result in overt phenotypes, sensitized screening in embryos depleted of grhl3 revealed 529 

genetic synergies. For instance, simultaneous reduction of grhl3 with tead3b or with 530 

klf17 mimicked the severe irf6 maternal mutant phenotype suggesting these factors 531 

cooperate within a tightly regulated hierarchy to ensure periderm integrity. Klf17 has 532 

previously been implicated in zebrafish periderm differentiation 83,84 , and Tead3 binding 533 

sites are enriched in periderm enhancers 73 . Simultaneous reduction of grhl3 and of 534 

grhl1 recapitulated the phenotype of grhl3 loss of function mutants, and simultaneous 535 

reduction of grhl3 and klf6a led to blebbing and rupture. These functional tests confirm 536 

the ability of TRN analysis to discover novel regulators of cellular differentiation. 537

A second strength of the study was the use of gold-standard datasets to benchmark 538 

network performance, which showed that incorporating chromatin accessibility as prior 539 

evidence yielded only a modest gain in network performance over models based solely 540 

on correlated gene expression. In a previous study also using gold-standard datasets 541 

> 44

, a position weight matrix (PWM)-based prior yielded a stronger improvement in 542 

network performance. The lesser benefit of prior in our study may reflect, first, that 543 

ATAC-seq data from single cells are inherently sparse, and the number of periderm 544 

cells we evaluated (i.e., several hundred) may have been insufficient to reveal all the 545 

periderm cis-regulatory elements; we believe our efforts to use SCENIC+ were foiled for 546 

this reason 85 . Second, PWMs for TF binding sites were available for only 151 of the 547 

226 TFs expressed in EVL, whereas the study mentioned above was conducted in 548 

human cells where the catalog of PWMs is more complete. Third, a given PWM can 549 

correspond to more than one TF, lessening the value of the prior matrix for inferring 550 

edges. This issue may have a bigger effect in teleost fish than in mammals because of 551 

a genome duplication event in the former 86 . Finally, PWMs are a poor proxy for TF 552 

peaks, because they are present in the genome in higher numbers than corresponding 553 

ChIP-seq peaks and, conversely, because they are not present beneath every ChIP-seq 554 

peak 87 . New machine learning tools can infer TF binding events from DNA sequence 555 

with better accuracy than PWMs 88 ; these tools may improve the value of incorporating 556 

prior data in TRN modeling. Despite the limitations of PWM-based prior, a recent study 557 

utilizing machine learning to infer PWMs for zebrafish TFs and relying heavily on prior 558 

evidence accurately captured the importance of Irf6 in the EVL TRN 78 , while our EVL 559 

TRN models, which relied primarily on correlated-gene-expression evidence, did not. 560
