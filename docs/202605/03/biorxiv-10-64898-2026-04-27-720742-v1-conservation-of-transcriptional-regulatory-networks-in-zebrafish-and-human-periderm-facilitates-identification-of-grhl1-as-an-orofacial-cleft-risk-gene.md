## 摘要
大多数口面裂（OFC）的遗传风险尚未归因于特定的基因或位点。IRF6 和 GRHL3 是两个已确定的 OFC 风险基因，它们编码的转录因子（TFs）对于周皮的分化至关重要；周皮是一种次级腭融合所需的瞬时胚胎组织。为了鉴定新的候选风险基因，我们构建了斑马鱼周皮转录调控网络（TRN）模型。利用盾状期胚胎的单细胞多组学测序（RNA-seq 和 ATAC-seq），我们通过整合基因表达相关性与周皮细胞开放染色质元件内的转录因子结合位点预测，推断了转录因子与靶基因之间的连接。我们通过对转录因子缺失的胚胎进行 RNA-seq 以及对野生型胚胎进行 ChIP-seq/CUT&RUN，生成了一组金标准边，并将其用于基准测试模型性能。在性能最佳的模型中，斑马鱼周皮模块在人类胚胎周皮中高度保守，且人类 OFC 相关基因的同源基因比非相关基因具有更高的中心度和边权重总和（edge-sum）得分。功能验证证实了该网络的预测能力：在敏感胚胎中敲低包括 grhl1、klf6a、tead3b 和 klf17 在内的高中心度转录因子，会破坏周皮分化。此外，对 2,415 个 OFC 家系的全基因组测序数据分析发现了 15 名携带罕见或新发 GRHL1 变异的个体，其中 4 个变异引入了提前终止密码子。本研究确定了 GRHL1 是一个新的 OFC 风险基因，并突显了跨物种基因调控网络分析在复杂结构性出生缺陷中优先筛选罕见变异候选基因的能力。

## Abstract
Most heritable risk for orofacial clefts (OFC) remains unassigned to specific genes or loci. IRF6 and GRHL3, two established OFC risk genes, encode transcription factors (TFs) essential for the differentiation of the periderm, a transient embryonic tissue required for secondary palate fusion. To identify novel risk candidates, we modeled the zebrafish periderm transcriptional regulatory network (TRN). Using single-cell multiome sequencing (RNA-seq and ATAC-seq) from shield-stage embryos, we inferred TF-to-target gene connections by integrating correlated gene expression with TF binding site predictions within chromatin elements open in periderm cells. We generated sets of gold-standard edges by conducting RNA-seq on TF-depleted embryos and ChIP-seq/CUT&RUN on wild-type embryos and used them to benchmark model performance. Within the top-performing model, zebrafish periderm modules are strongly preserved in human embryonic periderm and orthologs of human OFC-associated genes have higher centrality and edge-sum scores than non-associated genes. Functional validation confirmed the network's predictive power: depleting high-centrality TFs, including grhl1, klf6a, tead3b, and klf17, disrupted periderm differentiation in sensitized embryos. Moreover, analysis of whole-genome sequencing data from 2,415 OFC trios identified 15 individuals with rare or de novo GRHL1 variants, four of which introduced premature stop codons. This study establishes GRHL1 as a novel OFC risk gene and highlights the power of cross-species gene regulatory network analysis to prioritize candidates for rare variants in complex structural birth defects.
