Abstract 30 

Drug repurposing offers a cost -effective strategy to accelerate therapeutic discovery , but most 31 

computational approaches fail to model noncoding genetic variation . Because over 90% of 32 

genome -wide association study ( GWAS ) risk variants reside in noncoding regions, linking 33 

regulatory variation to therapeutic hypotheses remains a major challenge. Here, w e developed 34 

an integrative deep learning framework that links allele -specific enhancer predictio n to 35 

transcription factor (TF) -centered gene expression changes and drug -induced transcriptional 36 

profiles to prioritize candidate therapeutics. Our cell type -specific deep learning enhancer 37 

models accurately distinguish active enhancers across seven cell lines . Using breast cancer as 38 

a proof -of -concept , we found that GWAS heritability is significantly enriched in MCF7 39 

enhancer s, supporting MCF 7 as the cellular context for this disease . Allele -specific variant 40 

scoring identified breast cancer risk variants with strong allele -dependent effects , and 41 

attribution -based motif discovery revealed enrichment of FOXA1 -associated motif features, 42 

consistent with FOXA1 upregulation in primary tumors . Integration of the FOXA1 knockdown -43 

induced and drug -induced gene expression profiles identified 63 candidate compounds for 44 

treatment of breast cancer , including 18 approved drugs , with recovery of the known breast 45 

cancer therapy fulvestrant. Among prioritized compounds, 54% showed anti -correlated 46 

transcriptional effects across eight core breast cancer pathways , compared to 5.3% of non -47 

prioritized compounds . Integration of drug -gene interaction data further refined these to eight 48 

compounds with supporting experimental or clinical evidence. Together, t hes e results establish 49 

a regulatory variant -guided drug repurposing framework that connects noncoding genetic 50 

variation to therapeutic candidates and provides a generalizable strategy for translating the 51 

noncoding genome into pharmacologically relevant hypotheses. 52

Background 57 

The development of new therapeutics remains slow, costly, and characterized by high attrition 58 

rates [1 -5] . Traditional drug discovery typically requires 10 -17 years and cost s over $2 billion per 59 

approved drug, with only approximately 11% of compounds entering clinical trials ultimately 60 

receiv ing approval, and success rates even lower in areas such as neurodegenerative disease s61 

[6] . Drug repurposing offers a complementary strategy by identifying new indications for 62 

compounds with established safety profiles, thereby potentially accelerating therapeutic 63 

discovery while reducing cost and risk [7, 8] . A growing number of computational approaches 64 

have been developed to facilitate drug repurposing, including gene expression signature 65 

matching, network -based inference, and structure -based modeling [1, 9] . Many of these 66 

strategies leverage molecular phenotypes , such as drug -induced or disease -associated 67 

transcriptional profiles , to identify compounds with opposing or corrective effects, and have 68 

yielded promising results across diverse disease contexts, such as muscle atrophy [10] ,69 

neurodegenerative disorders [11, 12] , and infectious diseases [13] . However, most existing 70 

frameworks rely primarily on transcriptional anti -correlation or network similarity and lack explicit 71 

modeling of how genetic risk perturbs disease -relevant regulatory programs. 72 

Genome -wide association studies (GWAS) have revealed that over 90% of risk variants for 73 

complex traits reside in noncoding regions of the genome, particularly within distal regulatory 74 

elements such as enhancers [14 -16] . Enhancers orchestrate cell type -specific transcriptional 75 

programs through cooperative binding of TF s, and perturbation of enhancer activity represents a 76 

major mechanism by which genetic variation influences disease risk [17] . However, most 77 

GWAS -informed drug discovery approaches prioritize genes based on genomic proximity or 78 

statistical association without explicitly modeling allele -specific regulatory effects within 79 

enhancers or the TF -associated gene expression changes through which these variants 80 

influence disease risk [18, 19] . Consequently, how noncoding genetic risk variants can be 81 

translated into therapeutic hypotheses through their regulatory effects on gene expression 82 

remains poorly defined. 83 

Recent advances in deep learning -based regulatory modeling enable prediction of enhancer 84 

activity directly from DNA sequence and inference of allele -specific effects of noncoding variants 85 

[20] . These models provide a framework for mov ing beyond static association signals toward 86 

identif ication of TF -centered gene expression changes associated with genetic risk [21 -25] . We 87 

therefore hypothesize that disease -associated noncoding variants disproportionately affect 88 

enhancer sequence features associated with specific TFs, and that compounds whose drug -89 

induced gene expression changes directionally concordant with TF -associated gene expression 90 

changes represent computationally supported candidates for therapeutic repurposing .91 

Here, we present an integrative framework that moves from noncoding genetic risk to 92 

therapeutic prioritization by modeling allele -specific enhancer prediction , inferring disease -93 

relevant TF s, and matching TF knockdown -induced gene expression to drug -induced 94 

transcriptional responses. Our approach combines cell type -specific deep learning -based 95 

enhancer prediction, allele -specific variant impact assessment , and motif discovery to identify 96 

trait -relevant TFs, integration of TF knockdown -induced gene expression with drug -induced 97 

transcriptional responses to quantify directional concordance ; and pathway -level anti -correlation 98

Results 109 

Overview of the framework 110 

The overall analytical framework is summarized in Fig. 1. We first define d cell type -specific 111 

enhancers by integrating chromatin accessibility (ATAC -seq or DNase -seq) and H3K27ac 112 

profiles across multiple cell lines obtained from ENCODE [26] . Cell type -specific enhancer 113 

models were trained using TREDNet, a two -phase deep learning framework [27] . Phase one 114 

was pre -trained on 4560 genomic and epigenomic profiles from ENCODE v4 [28] , and p hase 115 

two models were fine -tuned for each cell type using 2 -kb enhancer and control sequences (see 116 

Methods). Using this model, GWAS variants were evaluated for allele -dependent differences in 117 

enhancer prediction, and candidate regulatory variants were identified based on allele -specific 118 

enhancer predictions . Attribution -based motif discovery (DeepLIFT [29] followed by TF -MoDISco 119 

[30] ) was then applied to infer TF motif features preferentially associated with risk alleles. 120 

To connect these regulatory features to downstream gene expression, TF knockdown -induced 121 

transcriptional profiles are integrated with drug -induced gene expression data from the LINCS 122 

L1000 dataset [31] . Compounds are prioritized based on transcriptional concordance with TF 123 

perturbation profiles from KnockTF 2.0 [32] , followed by pathway -level anti -correlation analysis 124 

and integration of curated drug -gene interaction data from DGIdb 5.0 [33] to refine candidate 125 

therapeutics. 126

Cell -specific e nhancer models were trained using the TREDNet two -phase deep learning 136 

framework [27] , achieving robust performance on chromosome -level held -out test sets (see 137 

Methods), with AUROC of 0.91 and AUPRC of 0. 65 in MCF7 cell line ( Fig. S1A -B). These 138 

results indicate reliable discrimination of enhancers from human open chromatin regions not 139 

overlapping enhancer sites, using a 1:10 positive -to -negative sampling ratio. 140 

Significant heritability enrichment was observed for trait -cell line pairs consistent with known cell 141 

type identity . MCF7 enhancer annotations span 2.4% of HapMap3 SNPs yet account for 25.1% 142 

of breast cancer SNP heritability (10.5 -fold enrichment ; p = 4.1 × 10 ⁻⁶; one -sided z -test with 143 

block jackknife standard error ; linkage disequilibrium score regression (LDSC ) partitioned 144 

heritability regression [34] ; Fig. S1 C), indicating that breast cancer genetic risk is 145 

disproportionately concentrated within MCF7 -specific regulatory elements, consistent with 146 

MCF7's origin as a breast cancer -derived cell line [35] . Based on this enrichment and the 147 

availability of matched TF perturbation and drug transcriptional data, we selected the MCF7 -148 

breast cancer pair for subsequent allele -specific modeling, TF inference, and drug prioritization 149 

analyses , as a proof -of -principle model .150 

Allele -specific TF convergence at breast cancer risk loci 151 

Focusing on the MCF7 breast cancer context, 20,619 breast cancer -associated variants passing 152 

the genome -wide significance threshold (p < 5 × 10 ⁻⁸) from full GWAS summary statistics [36] 153 

were used. FUMA mapped these variants to 151 genomic risk loci. To summarize their broader 154 

genomic distribution, we also intersected them with a predefined GRCh38 European LD block 155 

map (https://github.com/jmacdon/LDblocks_GRCh38/blob/master/data/pyrho_EUR_LD_blocks.bed ), 156 

which assigned 18,800 variants to 141 LD blocks , with many variants falling within the same 157 

block. Of the 20,619 significant variants , 830 variants overlapped MCF7 enhancer s (1 -kb 158 

windows). For each variant, we computed an allele -specific enhancer score difference ( Δscore), 159 

defined as the difference in TREDNet -predicted enhancer probability between the risk and 160 

protective allele sequences centered on a 2 -kb window ( Δscore = P risk allele − P protective allele ; see 161 

Methods). Variants were retained as candidate regulatory variants if at least one allele 162 

exceeded the model -derived enhancer probability threshold (false positive rate < 10%) and 163 

|Δ score| ranked within the top 10th percentile of the empirical distribution across all evaluated 164 

variants. This filtering identified 1,537 variants (7.5%) exhibiting strong allele -dependent 165 

differences in predicted enhancer probability, with risk alleles associated with both increases 166 

and decreases in enhancer prediction score, consistent with the heterogeneous regulatory 167 

architecture of complex trait loci ( Fig. S2C ). To identify TF motifs preferentially binding by breast 168 

cancer risk alleles through allele -specific differences in enhancer prediction, we applied 169 

attribution -based motif discovery to the 1,537 candidate regulatory variants. DeepLIFT [29] , a170 

feature attribution method that estimates the contribution of each input base to a neural network 171 

prediction relative to a reference input , was used to compute nucleotide -resolution contribution 172

Drug candidate prioritization through FOXA1 -centered transcriptional concordance 220 

To identify such compounds , we integrated the FOXA1 knockdown -induced gene expression 221 

profile from the MCF7 cell line (3,090 differentially expressed genes; FDR adjusted p < 0.05 222 

from differential expression analysis, as reported in KnockTF 2.0 ) with drug -induced 223 

transcriptional profiles from the LINCS L1000 dataset. For each compound in LINCS L1000, we 224 

computed a FOXA1 knockdown concordance score, defined as the mean drug -induced z -score 225 

of FOXA1 knockdown -upregulated genes minus the mean drug -induced z -score of FOXA1 226 

knockdown -downregulated genes, such that positive values indicate transcriptional 227 

concordance with FOXA1 knockdown (see Methods). Candidate compounds were required to 228 

satisfy two criteria: (i) statistically significant enrichment between drug -induced gene expression 229 

changes and FOXA1 knockdown -associated differentially expressed gene sets , and (ii) a 230 

FOXA1 knockdown concordance score within the top 10th percentile across all evaluated 231 

compounds. Approved drug enrichment was maximal and statistically significant exclusively at 232 

the 90th percentile threshold (2.1 -fold; odds ratio = 2.5; p = 0.002, one -sided Fisher's exact 233 

test), declining and losing significance at more stringent or more lenient thresholds (Table S2). 234 

This filtering yielded 63 candidate compounds, of which 18 are approved for human clinical use 235 

by the FDA or equivalent regulatory agencies according to DrugCentral [41] (a curated database 236 

of international drug approval status ) and ChEMBL [42] (a curated database of bioactive 237 

compounds with clinical development phase annotations ), represent ing a 2.1 -fold enrichment 238 

relative to the full LINCS L1000 library ( odds ratio = 2. 5; p = 0.00 2, one -sided Fisher's exact 239 

test) . To stratify candidates by clinical evidence , compounds were categorized into three tiers: 240 

Tier 1 - approved drugs with registered breast cancer clinical trials in ClinicalTrials.gov [43] ; Tier 241 

2- approved drugs without breast cancer -specific trials; and Tier 3 -investigational compounds 242 

(with or without breast cancer clinical trials ).243

Discussion 462 

In this study, we developed an integrative computational framework that connects disease -463 

associated noncoding varia nts to drug repurposing candidates by linking allele -specific 464 

enhancer prediction differences to disease -associated transcriptional changes and drug -induced 465 

transcriptional responses, using FOXA1 -centered gene expression changes in breast cancer as 466 

a proof -of -concept application . W e show that candidate regulatory variants preferentially alter 467 

enhancer prediction scores at loci enriched for FOXA1 -associated motif features . In addition, 468 

compounds whose transcriptional effects are concordant with FOXA1 knockdown -induced gene 469 

expression exhibit anti -correlated transcriptional changes to core breast cancer -associated 470 

hallmark pathways . Together, t hese findings establish a computational link between noncoding 471 

genetic risk, TF -centered gene expression , and pharmacologically relevant candidate 472 

compounds .473

A central biological finding from our analysis is the convergence of breast cancer risk variants 474 

on FOXA1 -associated motif features . FOXA1 functions as a pioneer TF that establishes 475 

accessible chromatin states in breast cancer and coordinates estrogen receptor -dependent 476 

transcriptional programs [39] . Aberrant FOXA1 activity has been implicated in endocrine -477 

resistant and metastatic ER -positive breast cancer [38, 63] . In our analysis, risk alleles showed 478 

strong preferential enrichment of FOXA1 -associated motif features among 32 evaluated TF 479 

motifs ( 40 vs. 0 seqlets in risk vs. protective alleles ), and FOXA1 showed strong upregulation 480 

among risk allele -enriched TFs in TCGA -BRCA primary tumors (log ₂FC = 2.18; q-value = 5.9 × 481 

10 ⁻¹⁶⁷ , DESeq2 Wald test ). Together, these observations support a model in which dispersed 482 

noncoding variants converge on lineage -defining enhancer -associated regulatory programs 483 

linked to breast cancer -relevant transcriptional changes .484 

Importantly, TF motifs identified through TF -MoDISco reflect motif features prioritized by the 485 

enhancer prediction model based on attribution scores , rather than direct evidence of differential 486 

TF binding in vivo between alleles [29] . Enrichment of a motif in one allele therefore indicates a 487 

stronger model -predicted regulatory contribution within that sequence context but does not 488 

imply absence of binding capacity or functional engagement in the alternate allele. This 489 

distinction is particularly relevant for pioneer factors such as FOXA1, which often bind broadly 490 

across accessible chromatin landscapes. Accordingly, our interpretation emphasizes allele -491 

dependent differences in model -predicted regulatory contribution rather than strict presence -492 

absence of binding events. 493 

While FOXA1 emerged as the strongest convergent factor based on allele -specific motif 494 

enrichment, gene expression in primary breast tumors , and functional evidence, enhancer 495 

activity is typically governed by cooperative binding of multiple TFs , with regulatory output 496 

arising from their combined interactions rather than a single dominant factor [64] . In addition to 497 

FOXA1 -associated motif enrichment, downstream drug perturbation analyses revealed 498 

coordinated changes in gene expression associated with additional TF -regulated gene sets ,499 

including TFAP2C -, RARA -, and KDM3A -associated gene expression changes . TFAP2C, 500 

RARA, and KDM3A have established roles in estrogen receptor -dependent and breast cancer -501 

associated gene expression [46, 47, 65] , suggesting that prioritized compounds modulate a 502 

broader set of co -regulatory TF -associated transcriptional programs beyond FOXA1 alone. 503 

Within such a combinatorial enhancer framework, discordance between allele -specific motif 504 

enrichment and gene expression in primary breast tumors for certain TFs , such as RXRG and 505 

BATF3, is biologically plausible. For example, RXRG functions as a tumor suppressor frequently 506 

silenced in breast cancer, whereas BATF3 is primarily associated with immune cell regulation 507 

[48, 66, 67] . Motif enrichment may therefore reflect variant -driven changes in local sequence 508 

context that alter the relative contribution of individual TFs to enhancer prediction , without 509 

necessarily changing their bulk expression levels in tumor tissue . This pattern is consistent with 510 

the principle that multiple TFs cooperatively regulate enhancer activity , such that changes in 511 

individual TF contributions do not require concordant changes in bulk expression levels [68, 69] .512 

Accordingly, drug prioritization was guided by directional concordance with FOXA1 knockdown -513 

induced gene expression changes , reflecting modulation of a central regulatory axis rather than 514 

inhibition of FOXA1 in isolation. 515

Methodologically, this work extends regulatory deep learning approaches beyond variant 535 

annotation toward drug repurposing . Previous drug repurposing frequently rel ied on global 536 

transcriptional anti -correlation or pathway overlap without explicit modeling of upstream 537 

regulatory perturbation. By integrating enhancer -based allele -specific modeling, TF knockdown -538 

induced gene expression profiles , directional transcriptional concordance scoring , and pathway -539 

level anti -correlation analysis , our framework incorporates upstream regulatory context into 540 

compound prioritization . This stepwise integration provides a quantitative strategy for translating 541 

noncoding genetic architecture into computationally supported therapeutic hypotheses. 542 

This framework can be generalized to any disease with related matched data available. We 543 

have explored several other cell lines, and we obtained decent performance for cell specific 544 

enhancer models and identified paired disease - cell line. Of 50 cell lines with overlapping 545 

LINCS and KnockTF coverage, seven retained sufficient epigenomic annotation after excluding 546 

cell lines with incomplete chromatin accessibility or H3K27ac profiles, or with documented prior 547 

perturbations including chemical treatment or physical manipulation such as ultrasonic 548 

treatment: A673, HCT116, HepG2, IMR90, K562, MCF7, and PC3 (see Methods). For each cell 549 

line, we defined enhancers by intersecting chromatin accessibility peaks with H3K27ac signals, 550

0.58 -0.86 across all seven cell lines (Fig. S1A -B). These results indicate reliable discrimination 556 

of enhancers from human open chromatin regions not overlapping enhancer sites, using a 1:10 557

Several limitations warrant consideration. First, inference of candidate regulatory variant s and 568 

compound prioritization are computational and require experimental validation to confirm 569 

regulatory and therapeutic effects. Second, applicability of the framework is constrained by the 570 

availability and overlap of public epigenomic, TF perturbation, and drug -induced transcriptional 571 

datasets, limiting analyzable cellular contexts. Third, drug -gene interaction database s (e.g. 572 

DGIdb 5.0 [33] ) are incomplete and not cell type -specific, which may obscure context -573 

dependent regulatory relationships. Fourth , the use of cancer -derived cell lines introduces 574 

somatic alterations that may not fully reflect germline regulatory mechanisms. Additionally, 575 

enhancer prediction models rely on available epigenomic annotations and may not fully capture 576 

regulatory dynamics under disease -specific conditions . These model s predict enhancer 577 

probability rather than direct enhancer activity, and allele -specific differences in prediction 578 

score s do not constitute direct evidence of differential regulatory function in vivo .579 

Future work should prioritize experimental validation of prioritized regulatory variants and 580

complex traits. Extending this framework to genetically driven neurological, immunological, and 583

regulatory modeling as a foundation for translating noncoding genetic risk into computationally 586

To quantify directional concordance between drug -induced and TF knockdown -associated 674 

transcriptional responses, a TF knockdown concordance score was calculated as: 675 

KD concordance = Mean (z -up) – Mean (z -down) 676 

where Mean(z_up) and Mean(z_down) represent the average drug -induced z -scores for genes 677 

up -regulated and down -regulated in the TF knockdown -induced differentially expressed gene 678 

set , respectively. This formulation follows the connectivity score principle of the connectivity map 679 

framework [77] , adapted to quantify directional concordance with TF knockdown -induced gene 680 

expression changes . For drugs with multiple perturbation profiles, the median concordance 681 

score across experiments was used. Statistical significance of the KD concordance score was 682 

assessed using an empirical permutation test that preserves the sizes of the TF knockdown up -683 

and down -regulated gene sets. For each drug -TF pair, random gene sets of equal sizes 684 

(number of up - and down -regulated genes ) were sampled without replacement from the L1000 685 

landmark gene universe, and the concordance score was recomputed across 5,000 686 

permutations to generate a null distribution. P -values were adjusted for multiple testing using 687 

the Benjamini -Hochberg FDR procedure. 688 

Concordance score distributions were compared between prioritized compound tiers and the full 689 

LINCS background using one -sided Mann -Whitney U tests. Pairwise comparisons between tiers 690 

were performed using two -sided Mann -Whitney U tests. 691

10th percentile threshold was selected empirically based on maximization of approved drug 696 

enrichment across threshold values spanning the 75th to 95th percentile .697
