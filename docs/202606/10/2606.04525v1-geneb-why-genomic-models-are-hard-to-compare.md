## 摘要
由于零散的基准测试、不兼容的评估协议以及特定任务的报告，基因组基础模型的进展难以评估。因此，关于模型优越性或通用性的声明往往无法直接比较。我们推出了GENEB，这是一个大规模诊断性基准测试，在统一的基于探针的协议（包括少样本场景）下，评估了40个基因组基础模型在跨越13个功能类别的100个任务中的冻结表示。GENEB使得在模型规模、架构、分词策略和预训练数据方面进行受控比较成为可能，同时明确暴露了任务层面的权衡。我们的分析表明，综合排行榜是不稳定的：模型排名在不同任务类别之间差异显著，规模带来的收益仅是适度的且不一致，而架构和预训练的对齐往往超过参数数量的影响。这些结果揭示了当前评估实践的局限性，并将GENEB定位为基因组机器学习中进行原则性比较和类别感知模型选择的参考框架。

## Abstract
Progress in genomic foundation models is difficult to assess due to fragmented benchmarks, incompatible evaluation protocols, and task-specific reporting. As a result, claims of superiority or generality across models are often not directly comparable. We introduce GENEB, a large-scale diagnostic benchmark that evaluates frozen representations from 40 genomic foundation models across 100 tasks spanning 13 functional categories under a unified probing-based protocol, including few-shot regimes. GENEB enables controlled comparison across model scale, architecture, tokenization, and pretraining data while explicitly exposing task-level trade-offs. Our analysis shows that aggregate leaderboards are unstable: model rankings vary sharply across task categories, scale provides only modest and inconsistent gains, and architectural and pretraining alignment frequently outweigh parameter count. These results highlight limitations of current evaluation practices and position GENEB as a reference framework for principled comparison and category-aware model selection in genomic machine learning.
