## 摘要
摘要 背景 将远端调控元件与其靶基因连接起来是解释染色质可及性及其他非编码基因组数据的一个核心问题。基于邻近性的映射方法简便，但忽略了三维增强子-启动子结构，可能会错误分配长距离调控效应。基于相关性的方法也可能因统计功效有限以及严格的距离或显著性阈值而遗漏调控连接。单细胞和单核多组学数据集，例如同时在同一细胞或细胞核中测量染色质可及性和基因表达的10x Multiome图谱，现在提供了一种通过测试连接的可及性特征预测基因表达的效果来评估基因-峰连接策略的方法。现有方法通常侧重于对单独的增强子-基因对进行评分。在本研究中，我们提出并构建了一个基于注释的快速候选基因-峰网络，并测试其是否改善下游基因表达的预测。方法 我们首先通过整合基于增强子、基于启动子和基于邻近性的连接策略，构建了一个统一的基因-峰调控网络。然后，我们使用来自肾脏精准医学项目（KPMP）10x Multiome队列的单细胞多组学数据，评估所提出的连接是否捕获了调控信号。我们在细胞类型簇水平上聚合了RNA表达和ATAC可及性数据，并训练预测模型来评估不同连接策略基于可及性解释基因表达的程度。在严格筛选的1,704个基因集和具有更宽松要求的适应性筛选的7,973个基因集中，通过测试R平方和均方误差（MSE）比较了基于注释（包括基于增强子和基于启动子的连接）和基于邻近性的基因-峰连接模型的性能。结果 在严格模式下（1,704个基因，每个基因至少由最近的TSS规则分配20个峰，且至少有10个基于注释的增强子峰），基于注释的模型始终比基于邻近性的模型获得更高的测试R平方和更低的测试MSE。在更大的适应性模式下（7,973个基因，每个基因至少有5个基于邻近性的峰和至少2个基于增强子的峰），我们为每个基因定义了基于其可用的最近和增强子连接的平衡数量的选定峰；基于注释的模型再次显示出全局更高的测试R平方和更低的测试MSE。在广泛的候选连接峰数量范围内都观察到了这些改进。结论 使用人类肾脏10x Multiome数据，我们证明了与传统方法相比，一个基于注释的快速基因-峰连接框架可以改善从染色质可及性预测基因表达的性能。这些结果支持在构建候选基因调控网络时使用生物学信息的增强子和启动子注释。我们的框架还与基于相关性的Signac LinkPeaks方法一致，同时提供了更广泛的覆盖范围和更高的计算效率。我们已在GPlinksR R包中实现了这些基于注释的连接方法，为构建调控网络提供了一个快速且可扩展的工具。

## Abstract
Abstract Background Linking distal regulatory elements to their target genes is a central problem for interpreting chromatin accessibility and other non-coding genomic data. Proximity-based mapping is convenient but ignores three-dimensional enhancer-promoter architecture and can misassign long-range regulatory effects. Correlation-based approaches can also miss regulatory links because of limited statistical power and restrictive distance or significance thresholds. Single-cell and single-nucleus multi-omic datasets, such as 10x Multiome profiles that jointly measure chromatin accessibility and gene expression in the same cells or nuclei, now provide a way to evaluate gene-peak linkage strategies by testing how well linked accessibility features predict gene expression. Existing methods often focus on scoring individual enhancer-gene pairs. In this study, we proposed and constructed a fast annotation-based candidate gene-peak network and tested whether it improves downstream prediction of gene expression. Methods We first built a unified gene-peak regulatory network by integrating enhancer-based, promoter-based, and proximity-based linkage strategies. We then used single-cell multiome data from the Kidney Precision Medicine Project (KPMP) 10x Multiome cohort to evaluate whether the proposed links captured regulatory signals. We aggregated RNA expression and ATAC accessibility at the cell-type cluster level and trained predictive models to evaluate how well different linkage strategies could explain gene expression based on accessibility. Model performance was compared between annotation-based (including both enhancer- and promoter-based links) and proximity-based gene-peak links using testing R-squared and mean squared error (MSE) in a strict set of 1,704 genes and an adaptive set of 7,973 genes with more relaxed requirements. Results In the strict regime (1,704 genes with at least 20 peaks assigned by the nearest-TSS rule and at least 10 annotated enhancer-based peaks), the annotation-based model consistently achieved higher testing R-squared and lower testing MSE than the proximity-based model. In the larger adaptive regime (7,973 genes with at least 5 proximity-based and at least 2 enhancer-based peaks), we defined for each gene a balanced number of selected peaks based on its available closest and enhancer links; the annotation-based model again showed globally higher testing R-squared and lower testing MSE. These improvements were observed over a broad range of candidate-linked peak numbers. Conclusions Using human kidney 10x Multiome data, we show that a fast annotation-based gene-peak linkage framework can improve prediction of gene expression from chromatin accessibility compared with conventional approaches. These results support the use of biologically informed enhancer and promoter annotations when constructing candidate gene regulatory networks. Our framework also showed concordance with the correlation-based Signac LinkPeaks method while providing broader coverage and greater computational efficiency. We have implemented these annotation-based linkage methods in the GPlinksR R package, providing a fast and scalable tool for constructing regulatory networks.
