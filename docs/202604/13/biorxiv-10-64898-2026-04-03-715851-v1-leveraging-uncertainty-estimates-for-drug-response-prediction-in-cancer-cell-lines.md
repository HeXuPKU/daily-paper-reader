## 摘要
动机：利用癌症细胞系组学谱预测药物反应的机器学习模型有望推动精准肿瘤学的发展，但其效用受到预测质量异质性以及在分布偏移下发生“静默失败”的限制。不确定性量化可以应对这些挑战，但该领域尚缺乏对相关方法的系统性评估。结果：我们对七种用于药物反应预测的不确定性感知模型进行了基准测试，比较了通过集成差异衡量的认知不确定性（epistemic uncertainty）、通过分布建模衡量的偶然不确定性（aleatoric uncertainty）以及两者的结合。高斯神经网络集成能够可靠地标记出分布外（out-of-distribution）输入，并且在筛选出置信度最高的 10% 预测结果时，均方误差降低了 64%。我们讨论了概率预测如何实现考虑治疗相关反应范围的候选药物分析。通过不确定性归因，我们识别出了不可预测性的转录组特征，即与预测不确定性相关的基因。我们还证明了不确定性引导的主动学习可以优先处理具有信息量的实验。

## Abstract
MotivationMachine learning models that predict drug response from cancer cell line omics profiles could advance precision oncology, yet their utility is limited by heterogeneous prediction quality and silent failures under distribution shifts. Uncertainty quantification can address these challenges, but systematic evaluation of methods for this domain is lacking.

ResultsWe benchmark seven uncertainty-aware models for drug response prediction, comparing epistemic uncertainty via ensemble disagreement, aleatoric uncertainty via distributional modeling, and their combination. Gaussian neural network ensembles reliably flag out-of-distribution inputs and achieve a 64% reduction in mean squared error when filtering to the 10% most confident predictions. We discuss how probabilistic predictions can enable drug candidate analyses that account for therapeutically relevant response ranges. Through uncertainty attribution, we identify transcriptomic signatures of unpredictability, i.e., genes associated with prediction uncertainty. We also demonstrate that uncertainty-guided active learning can prioritize informative experiments.
