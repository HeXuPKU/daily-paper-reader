## 摘要
人类疾病和药物不良反应最终通过临床症状来识别，但大多数症状的分子决定因素仍不清楚。为解决这一关键问题，我们提出了 PHENOCAUZ，这是一个计算框架，通过整合孟德尔表型-基因关系与蛋白质的分子特征，将症状与其致病蛋白联系起来。PHENOCAUZ 从源自孟德尔表型及其致病基因的症状注释出发，识别与单个症状相关的生物通路和过程，并训练机器学习模型来预测除目前已知的孟德尔疾病相关蛋白之外的致病蛋白。该方法的动机在于以下假设：如果某种蛋白质的功能障碍在孟德尔遗传病中产生某种症状，那么该蛋白质也可能在复杂疾病中导致相同的症状，或引起药物毒性。通过对 2,344 种症状和 4,828 种孟德尔蛋白进行留一交叉验证（leave-one-out cross-validation）基准测试，结果显示前几名预测的估计准确率约为 0.70。预测的症状-蛋白质关系在通路水平上与文献整理的症状-蛋白质关联、有效药物靶点以及疾病作用机制蛋白表现出高度一致性。PHENOCAUZ 还支持实际应用，包括预测严重的药物副作用，以及识别卵巢癌、前列腺癌、乳腺癌以及痴呆症和克罗恩病等非癌症疾病的候选治疗药物。这些结果表明，孟德尔疾病生物学为连接临床症状与其分子决定因素提供了一条强有力的途径，并将这些见解转化为药物研发和安全性预测。

意义声明：临床症状定义了疾病和药物副作用的识别方式，但大多数症状的分子决定因素仍不清楚。我们推出了 PHENOCAUZ 框架，该框架利用孟德尔疾病的基因-表型关系，通过将症状与分子机制联系起来，推断导致人类症状的蛋白质和通路，从而实现对药物不良反应的预测，并为孟德尔疾病和复杂疾病发现潜在的治疗靶点。

## Abstract
Human diseases and adverse drug reactions are ultimately recognized through clinical symptoms, yet the molecular determinants of most symptoms remain unknown. To address this key issue, we present PHENOCAUZ, a computational framework that links symptoms to their causative proteins by integrating Mendelian phenotype-gene relationships with molecular features of proteins. Starting from symptom annotations derived from Mendelian phenotypes and their causal genes, PHENOCAUZ identifies biological pathways and processes associated with individual symptoms and trains a machine learning model to predict symptom-causing proteins beyond those currently implicated in Mendelian diseases. The approach is motivated by the hypothesis that if dysfunction of a protein produces a symptom in a Mendelian disorder, the same protein may contribute to the same symptom in a complex disease or cause drug toxicity. Benchmarking across 2,344 symptoms and 4,828 Mendelian proteins using leave-one-out cross-validation yielded an estimated precision of approximately 0.70 among the top predictions. Predicted symptom-protein relationships show strong pathway-level agreement with literature-curated symptom-protein associations, efficacious drug targets and disease mode-of-action proteins. PHENOCAUZ also enables practical applications including prediction of severe drug side effects and identification of candidate therapeutics for ovarian, prostate, and breast cancers as well as noncancer diseases such as dementia and Crohns disease. These results demonstrate that Mendelian disease biology provides a powerful route to connect clinical symptoms with their molecular determinants and translate those insights into drug discovery and safety prediction.

Significance StatementClinical symptoms define how diseases and drug side effects are recognized, yet the molecular determinants of most symptoms remain unknown. We introduce PHENOCAUZ, a framework that uses Mendelian disease gene-phenotype relationships to infer the proteins and pathways responsible for human symptoms, by linking symptoms to molecular mechanisms and enabling the prediction of adverse drug reactions and discovery of potential therapeutic targets for both Mendelian and complex diseases.
