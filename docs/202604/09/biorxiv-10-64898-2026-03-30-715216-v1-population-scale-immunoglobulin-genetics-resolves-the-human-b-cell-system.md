## 摘要
免疫球蛋白 (Ig) 介导适应性体液免疫，然而人类体内 B 细胞反应的调节机制仍难以通过直接实验进行研究。在此，我们利用群体规模的 Ig 遗传学来解析人类 B 细胞系统的分子调节。通过对 114,697 名个体的循环 IgA、IgG、IgM 以及六种复合 Ig 性状进行分析，共鉴定了 504 个遗传关联。结合调控基因组学、血浆蛋白质组学和免疫表型分析，我们将这些效应映射到 B 细胞层级结构中，不仅找回了已知的调节因子，还揭示了体液免疫中此前未被识别的基因。在关键控制节点——包括 Fcγ 受体、免疫球蛋白重链位点和 TACI-APRIL 信号轴——变异体形成了等位基因系列，对抗体输出产生分级扰动。Ig 相关位点与自身免疫、免疫缺陷和 B 细胞恶性肿瘤表现出广泛的重叠。这些发现表明，在群体规模上分析的 Ig 性状编码了关于人类 B 细胞系统调节的细粒度信息，并将体液免疫的自然变异与免疫介导的疾病联系起来。

## Abstract
Immunoglobulins (Ig) mediate adaptive humoral immunity, yet the regulation of B-cell responses in vivo in humans remains inaccessible to direct experimentation. Here we use population-scale Ig genetics to resolve molecular regulation of the human B-cell system. Analysis of circulating IgA, IgG, IgM, and six composite Ig traits in 114,697 individuals identifies 504 genetic associations. Integration with regulatory genomics, plasma proteomics, and immunophenotyping maps these effects across the B-cell hierarchy, recovering known regulators and revealing previously unrecognized genes in humoral immunity. At key control nodes - including Fc{gamma} receptors, the immunoglobulin heavy-chain locus and the TACI-APRIL signaling axis - variants form allelic series generating graded perturbations of antibody output. Ig-associated loci show extensive overlap with autoimmunity, immunodeficiency and B-cell malignancy. These findings demonstrate that Ig traits, analyzed at population scale, encode fine-grained information about the regulation of the human B-cell system and link natural variation in humoral immunity to immune-mediated disease.
