## 摘要
传染病被认为是人类历史上最强大的选择压力之一，对人类种群的遗传组成产生了深远影响。最近，针对免疫性状的大规模全基因组关联研究（GWAS）强调了这样一种观点：对传染病的遗传易感性可能源于人类基因组中数千个位点的共同作用。为了对这些性状的多基因遗传进行建模，可以将多个变异组合成多基因风险评分（PRS），用以评估个体对某种性状的遗传易感性。通过将来自欧洲大型生物样本库的现代 GWAS 统计数据与来自西欧亚大陆的 3,500 多名古代个体的基因组数据相结合，我们刻画了四个具有高遗传性的传染病相关性状在 10,000 年间的时间变化。在此过程中，我们考虑了这些性状在时间、空间和遗传祖源上的差异，并证明所观察到的模式无法仅用遗传漂变来解释。我们的研究结果表明，人类历史上的重大疾病爆发与人类种群中多基因性状的转变有关。具体而言，查士丁尼大瘟疫、安东尼大瘟疫和中世纪早期的麻疹爆发这三个事件，与这些性状多基因谱的显著转变相吻合。利用基因本体论（Gene Ontology）富集分析方法，我们展示了这些转变涉及多个系统性生物过程，并一致强调了直接或间接调节免疫反应的代谢途径。

## Abstract
Infectious diseases are recognized as one of the strongest selective forces, exerting a profound influence on the genetic makeup of human populations over time. Recently, large-scale genome-wide association studies (GWAS) on immunological traits have underscored the notion that genetic predisposition to infectious diseases likely stems from the contribution of several thousand loci across the human genome. To model the polygenic inheritance of these traits, multiple variants can be combined into polygenic risk scores (PRS), which estimate an individuals genetic predisposition for a trait. By combining present-day GWAS statistics from large European biobanks with genomic data from more than 3,500 ancient individuals from Western Eurasia, we characterize temporal changes in four highly heritable infectious disease-related traits over a span of 10,000 years. In doing so, we account for variation in these traits across time, space, and genetic ancestries, and demonstrate that the observed patterns cannot be explained by genetic drift alone. Our findings suggest that major disease outbreaks in human history are associated with shifts in polygenic traits in human populations. Specifically, three events - the Justinian Plague, Antonine Plague, and early medieval measles outbreaks - coincide with significant shifts in the polygenic profiles of these traits. Using a Gene Ontology enrichment approach, we show that these shifts involve multiple systemic biological processes, with a consistent emphasis on metabolic pathways modulating immunological responses both directly and indirectly.

*   **疫情驱动的遗传偏移**：PRS 的显著转变与历史记载的三大疫情高度吻合：**查士丁尼瘟疫（约 1,400 BP）**、**安东尼瘟疫（约 1,800 BP）**以及**中世纪早期的麻疹爆发（约 1,000 BP）**。

### 8. 不足与局限
*   **PRS 迁移性问题（Portability）**：PRS 模型是基于现代人群构建的，直接应用于古代样本可能存在偏差（由于连锁不平衡 LD 模式随时间改变）。
*   **数据质量限制**：古 DNA 的低覆盖度和伪单倍型处理可能导致部分遗传信息丢失，尤其是稀有变异。
