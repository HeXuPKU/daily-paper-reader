Abstract 15 

16 

Single-cell ATAC-sequencing enables high-resolution mapping of cell states and their putative 17 regulatory elements. The enrichment of genome-wide association study (GWAS) signals within 18 context-specific regulatory regions can reveal disease-relevant cell populations. We present 19 Single-Cell ATAC-seq Disease Score (SCADS), a computational framework that integrates 20 GWAS summary statistics with scATAC-seq data to prioritize disease-relevant cells. SCADS 21 produces calibrated, cell-level scores that are comparable across datasets and traits by: (i) 22 identifying chromatin co-regulatory regions via topic modeling, (ii) quantifying their polygenic 23 GWAS enrichment, and (iii) calculating cell-specific disease scores from topic weights and 24 enrichment levels. Across extensive simulations, SCADS outperforms existing methods in power 25 while controlling false positives. Applied to multiple autoimmune traits, SCADS reveals marked 26 heterogeneity in disease relevance within canonical immune cell types. We specifically studied 27 the differential disease relevance within CD8 + T and colon epithelial cells for inflammatory bowel 28 disease, and pinpointed underlying gene programs and variants. SCADS is scalable, 29 interpretable, and modular, providing a general framework to connect noncoding regulatory 30 variation to cellular identity and function. 31

Introduction 33 

34 Identifying the tissues and cell types that contribute to a disease is a crucial step toward 35 understanding disease mechanisms. Many complex diseases exhibit substantial heritability, 36 indicating that genetic variants can provide valuable insights into disease etiology 1. Genome -wide 37 association studies (GWAS) have successfully pinpointed thousands of loci associated with 38 disease 2–4 . Because the majority of disease -associated variants lie in regulatory regions, which 39 are often active in a cell -type or tissue -specific manner, integrating GWAS results with 40 regulatory -element annotations offers a powerful means of delineating disease -relevant cellular 41 contexts 5–7 .42

Moreover, analyses that 50 treat each cell type as homogeneous ignore inherent heterogeneity within cell types and may 51 therefore miss biologically important processes such as differentiation, activation, or 52 environmental responses 20,21 .53 54 The rapid accumulation of scATAC -seq data now provides unprecedented resolution of 55 chromatin accessibility for individual cells 17,22–24 . Consequently, there exists great potential to map 56 disease relevance to specific cellular states by integrating scATAC -seq with GWAS data. In 57 principle, if we knew the exact accessibility status of every regulatory element in an individual cell, 58 we could apply S-LDSC 8—as is routinely done for bulk ATAC-seq—to assess disease relevance. 59 Specifically, by treating the cell’s open regions as an annotation, S-LDSC could estimate the per-60 variant heritability enrichment for variants in open regions relative to closed regions, yielding a 61 disease score for that cell ( Fig. 1a ). In practice, this is hampered by the extreme sparsity of 62 scATAC -seq: A typical cell captures reads in only ~3 % (1-10%) of the regions that are detectable 63 in bulk 23,25 , leaving the majority of regulatory elements unobserved and limiting the power of 64 enrichment tests. 65 66 To overcome this limitation, several recent methods have been proposed. SCAVENGE 26 

67 evaluates the enrichment of fine -mapped GWAS variants in open regions per cell but retains this 68 enrichment as the disease relevance score only for the top -ranked cells (seed cells); the scores 69 for the remaining cells are inferred from their similarity to these top cells. This strategy is 70 constrained by the noisy, sparse information in individual cells and thus is prone to mistakes when 71 choosing seed cells. It also does not exploit the polygenic architecture of most traits. scPRS 72 computes a polygenic risk score for each cell based on read counts overlapping GWAS variants 73 and then smooths the scores using a cell -cell similarity graph 27 . However, scPRS requires 74 individual -level labeled training data, which may not be available for many traits. The scores 75 produced by scPRS are also not directly comparable across traits or datasets and lack calibrated 76 measures of uncertainty. 77 78 To address these shortcomings, we developed SCADS (Single -Cell ATAC-seq Disease 79 Score), a method that infers disease relevance at the resolution of individual cells. The key idea 80 is simple: We first project each cell onto a low -rank representation of its chromatin -accessibility 81 profile, perform polygenic enrichment analysis in this reduced space using GWAS summary 82 statistics, and then reconstruct a cell -level disease relevance score. It also simultaneously 83 produces the set of regulatory regions driving that relevance which can be used to fine-map 84 GWAS causal variants. Overview of SCADS . SCADS provides a cell -level score that reflects a cell’s disease 95 relevance, defined as the per -variant heritability in the open regions of the cell compared with 96 background. The essential idea is that, although it is difficult to infer open chromatin regions from 97 a single cell, learning the co -regulatory pattern of chromatin status across cells allows us to 98 reliably estimate the contribution of a few “open -region sets” in any cell; we can therefore obtain 99 the cell’s score by estimating the disease relevance of each “open -region set” and aggregate their 100 contributions ( Fig. 1b ). More specifically, in the first step, SCADS performs topic modeling on 101 scATAC -seq read -count data (see Methods); we employ the fastTopics 28,29 implementation for 102 computational efficiency. It then obtains topic loadings for each cell and defines an “open -region 103 set” for each topic (see Methods), which are groups of co -regulated regions underlying a 104 transcriptional program. In the second step, SCADS assesses disease relevance for each topic 105 by calculating the heritability enrichment for a GWAS trait using S-LDSC, treating the 106 corresponding “open -region set” as the functional annotation. Finally, SCADS constructs the 107 cell -level score as the expected heritability enrichment for variants in the cell’s open regions, 108 together with its variance and p -value (see Methods). The formula is intuitive given that the cell 109 score depends on its topic loadings, the size of each topic, and the disease relevance of each 110 topic. This workflow allows SCADS to overcome sparsity by projecting cells onto 111 lower -dimensional factors, assessing disease relevance at the latent -factor level, and 112 reconstructing the cell score. It leverages the polygenic trait signal for more powerful enrichment 113 detection and provides a score with an intuitive meaning that is comparable across datasets. 114 115 SCADS further leverages the cell -level score and topic -derived functional annotations for 116 downstream analysis ( Fig. 1b ). It uses the topic annotations and the learned enrichments as 117 priors for Bayesian fine -mapping, and the cell scores enable novel investigations of variant -trait 118 associations—for example, a positive correlation between the chromatin accessibility containing 119 the variant and SCADS cell scores indicates that up -regulation of the variant’s target gene or a 120 coregulated gene program is associated with the disease, thereby providing more mechanistic 121 insights into variant-trait associations. 122 123

SCADS assesses disease relevance for several autoimmune traits at cell level. We 190 applied SCADS on the hematopoiesis scATAC-seq data 18 for eight autoimmune traits: 191 Rheumatoid arthritis (RA), systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE), hypothyroidism (HTD), 192 inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), primary biliary cholangitis (PBC), type -1 diabetes (T1D), atopic 193 dermatitis (eczema), and asthma. We then visualized the heterogeneity of trait 196 relevance by calculating the difference between the 95th and 5th percentile (or range) of SCADS 197 score for each cell type ( Fig. 4b ). B cells, T cells and NK cells were the more relevant cell types 198 for most traits, but they also displayed substantial heterogeneity. Discussion 320 

321 

We developed SCADS, a computational framework that prioritizes disease-relevant cell 322 types and states by integrating GWAS summary statistics with scATAC-seq data. SCADS 323 generates a cell-level score that reflects the enrichment of disease heritability within the cell's 324 open chromatin regions. Extensive simulations demonstrated that SCADS can accurately identify 325 disease-relevant cells while effectively controlling for false positives. We demonstrated the utility 326 of SCADS by applying it to autoimmune traits, particularly IBD, leading to novel insights into the 327 disease's genetic etiology. 328 329 The extreme sparsity of scATAC -seq data hampers the cell -level inference of disease 330 relevance. Recent studies 59 have shown that it is virtually impossible to determine whether a given 331 chromatin region is open or closed in an individual cell. SCADS tackles this limitation by applying 332 topic modeling to produce a denoised, covariate adjusted, low -dimensional representation for 333 open or closed chromatin status and then performing cell-level inference from this low 334 dimensional space. Other cell level inference methods also perform aggregation in some format 335 to make cell-level inference. For example, SCAVENGE and g-chromVAR aggregate read counts 336 across GWAS causal variants and compare against the background for a cell. scPRS uses Latent 337 semantic indexing (LSI) to learn embeddings for individual cells to get a score. A few cell scoring 338 methods for single-cell RNA-seq or multiome have been developed 60–63 , aggregating signals at 339 disease gene sets or pathways. We found topic modeling is convenient for this application 340 because it learns the “topics” from scATAC-seq data, which are often coregulated genomic 341 regions with clear biological meanings. SCADS then reconstructs the cell-level score using the 342 weights of the topics for a cell (determined by reads distribution and topic open region size) and 343 the topic’s enrichment of GWAS signals compared to the background. The score has an explicit 344 meaning–it is the enrichment of GWAS signals in the open regions of cells compared to the 345 background. 346 347 We further demonstrate the utility of SCADS for uncovering disease -associated genetic 348 mechanisms. By leveraging topic -derived annotations, we can infer gene -regulatory programs 349

It is also possible to leverage mouse scATAC -seq datasets, which have proven to 363 be valuable surrogates for human regulatory landscapes. We will therefore assess the utility of 364 mouse scATAC -seq data for elucidating human traits in future studies. 365 366 In summary, we anticipate that SCADS will be broadly applicable to scATAC -seq profiles 367 across diverse cell states and experimental conditions, thereby facilitating the understanding of 368 the genetics of complex traits. 369
