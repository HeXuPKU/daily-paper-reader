## 摘要
尽管饮食摄入是许多常见疾病的主要风险因素，但对饮食建议的依从性仍然较低。这可能在一定程度上反映了对环境和遗传因素导致的饮食行为个体差异考虑不足。虽然饮食摄入的全基因组关联研究（GWAS）已鉴定出数百个相关位点，但X染色体在很大程度上被忽视了。为了填补这一空白，我们将多种全X染色体关联研究（X-WAS）模型应用于饮食摄入表型，以鉴定新的关联。我们对来自UK Biobank的多达424,758名欧洲参与者的食物频率问卷中的46种饮食摄入特征进行了X-WAS。表型包括定量测量（如水果摄入量）、二元特征（如脱咖啡因咖啡与含咖啡因咖啡）以及基于主成分分析得出的食物组。我们使用多种模型测试了遗传关联：传统的性别合并加性GWAS、按性别分层的加性模型，以及两个考虑性别相互作用效应和非加性效应的联合模型。我们还在另外五个遗传祖先群体中进行了X-WAS，并对多达445,773人进行了性别合并的多祖先加性GWAS荟萃分析。我们鉴定出18个与20种饮食摄入特征相关的位点（P < 5x10-8），其中包括17个在GWAS Catalog中此前没有关联记录的变异。在这些位点中，有10个在多个X-WAS模型中具有显著性，有5个在传统性别合并加性GWAS以外的模型中表现最强，这突显了处理X染色体已知复杂性的方法的价值。这些结果表明，在GWAS中纳入X染色体可以揭示新的位点，即使是对于饮食摄入等复杂的行为特征也是如此。应用多种关联模型通过考虑X染色体的独特特征，进一步提高了发现能力。

## Abstract
Although dietary intake is a leading risk factor for many common diseases, adherence to dietary recommendations remains low. This may partly reflect limited consideration of individual differences in eating behavior that arise from both environmental and genetic factors. While genome-wide association studies (GWAS) of dietary intake have identified hundreds of associated loci, the X chromosome has largely been ignored. To address this gap, we applied multiple X-chromosome-wide association study (X-WAS) models on dietary intake phenotypes to identify novel associations. We performed X-WAS of 46 dietary intake traits from food frequency questionnaires in up to 424,758 European participants from the UK Biobank. Phenotypes included quantitative measures (e.g., fruit intake), binary traits (e.g., decaffeinated vs caffeinated coffee), and principal component-derived food groups. We tested for genetic associations using several models: a traditional sex-combined additive GWAS, additive models stratified by sex, and two joint models accounting for sex-interaction effects and non-additivity. We also conducted X-WAS in five additional genetic ancestry groups and performed a sex-combined multi-ancestry additive GWAS meta-analysis with up to 445,773 individuals. We identified 18 loci associated with 20 dietary intake traits (P < 5x10-8), including 17 variants without prior associations in the GWAS Catalog. Among these loci, 10 were significant across multiple X-WAS models, and 5 were strongest in a model other than the traditional sex-combined additive GWAS, highlighting the value of approaches that address known complexities of the X chromosome. These results demonstrate that incorporating the X chromosome in GWAS can reveal novel loci, even for complex behavioral traits such as dietary intake. Applying multiple association models further improves discovery by accounting for unique features of the X chromosome.
